# Education Scorecard 2026 "Districts on the Rise" — ELA Curriculum & Outperformance Synthesis

Source: https://educationscorecard.org/districts-on-the-rise/. Harvard–Stanford–Dartmouth project. Qualifying districts must serve ≥1,200 students grades 3-8, have ≥4 demographically comparable peer districts in their state, and post demographic-adjusted achievement gains of **≥0.3 grade levels** in *both* 2019-2025 and 2022-2025. Districts with large enrollment/demographic shifts are excluded.

**Limitation:** Public case studies expose only binary "qualified" status plus a 0.3 grade-level floor. Per-district point estimates appear in aggregated press coverage only (e.g., Modesto = +13 weeks reading).

---

## (1) Reading-Only Districts — Deep Dive (3 districts)

### A. Modesto City Elementary SD, CA (Vanessa Buitrago)
- **Demographics:** 16,851 students; 83.9% economically disadvantaged; 72.1% Hispanic, 14.3% White; ~30%+ EL when reform began.
- **K-5 ELA stack:** **Benchmark Advance** (pre-existing) + **UFLI added** because teachers reported "the foundational skills were completely deficient in Benchmark"; **LETRS** training district-wide at ~$5,000/teacher introduced pre-pandemic.
- **Demographic-adjusted ELA gain (PUBLISHED):** **+13 extra weeks of reading learning** vs matched peers — one of only a handful of California high-FRPL districts above 2019 baseline.
- **SoR shift:** Yes — LETRS rolled out pre-pandemic; UFLI added because Benchmark Advance's phonics scope was insufficient.
- **Verdict for Troy:** Most precisely-measured Reading-Only outperformer. Stack is **Benchmark Advance + UFLI + LETRS** — almost parallel to Troy's tentative Collab Lit + UFLI, but **the driver was LETRS teacher knowledge, not the core curriculum**. Modesto's leaders said the core was deficient and had to be patched.
- Sources: [Case study PDF](https://educationscorecard.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/CA_Modesto-City-Schools-Case-Study.docx.pdf); [Chalkbeat](https://www.chalkbeat.org/2026/05/13/education-scorecard-shows-us-students-in-reading-recession/); [EdSource](https://edsource.org/2026/california-school-districts-math-reading/758170).

### B. West Baton Rouge Parish, LA (Chandler Smith)
- **Demographics:** 4,165 students; 72.8% econ-disadvantaged; 53.1% Black, 36.3% White, 8% Hispanic.
- **K-5 ELA stack:** **Wit & Wisdom (Great Minds)** + **Wilson Fundations K-3** (LA state-recommended SoR pairing). Adoption fall 2023; two-year rollout. Needs assessment identified "phonics and phonemic awareness as critical gaps."
- **ELA gain:** **K-3 literacy proficiency surged 57% → 68% in a single year** (2023-24 to 2024-25). District rose from #25 to #21 in LA ranking; top-25 statewide for highest-performance students.
- **SoR shift:** Implicit but strong — Louisiana's Act 108 mandate.
- **Verdict for Troy:** **Only Reading-Only district that uses Wit & Wisdom + Fundations** — directly parallel to one of Troy's strongest alternatives. Gain in one year of implementation.
- Sources: [Case study PDF](https://educationscorecard.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/LA_West-Baton-Rouge_Case-Study.pdf); [LA DOE W&W+Fundations+Geodes review](https://doe.louisiana.gov/docs/default-source/curricular-resources/great-minds-pbc---wit-wisdom-with-fundations-and-geodes-ela-grades-k-3-(-2023).pdf?sfvrsn=b5018413_5); [WBRZ coverage](https://www.wbrz.com/news/west-baton-rouge-parish-schools-says-district-looks-to-build-on-success-of-previous-school-year).

### C. Roanoke County Public Schools, VA (Ken Nicely)
- **Demographics:** 13,736 students; 36.1% econ-disadvantaged; 70.4% White, 9.6% Hispanic, 8.6% Black — **closest demographic match to Troy** in the Reading-Only group.
- **K-5 ELA stack:**
  - **Heggerty** (phonemic awareness, K-1)
  - **Really Great Reading** (phonics/decoding, K-3)
  - **Wit & Wisdom** (knowledge-building comprehension)
  - Dynamic Curriculum Guide K-10 — RCPS specifies named programs within a district-developed guide.
- **ELA gain:** **7th in Virginia for student reading performance, 6th in math** as of 2025.
- **SoR shift:** Yes — Virginia's Literacy Act (VLA) 2022 mandate. RCPS Division Literacy Plan "grounded in research on the science of reading."
- **Verdict for Troy:** **Single most directly comparable district to Troy.** Affluent suburban, majority-white VA county. Stack — **Heggerty + Really Great Reading + Wit & Wisdom** — is the cleanest "elite-suburban SoR" archetype on the list. Strongest single signal that an affluent district *does not* land on a workshop-style core.
- Sources: [Case study PDF](https://educationscorecard.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/VA_Roanoke-County_Case-Study.pdf); [RCPS literacy page](https://www.rcps.us/departments/english/literacy); [RCPS Core Reading Instruction](https://www.rcps.info/Page/1135).

## (2) Math+Reading Combined — ELA-Relevant Ranking (17 districts)

### Tier 1 — Strongest ELA Evidence

**1. Detroit Public Schools Community District (MI) — Nikolai Vitti**
- 49,038 students; 85% econ-disadvantaged; 80% Black, 16% Hispanic, 15% EL.
- **K-5 ELA: EL Education K-5 Language Arts** (selected 2017, rolled out 2018-19; modEL Detroit project, Skillman Foundation grant). 267 academic interventionists hired post-litigation settlement; 120-minute daily literacy block.
- "Outpaced the state" in Y1 of EL Education implementation; sustained through pandemic.
- **Direct relevance to Troy:** **Only in-state Michigan validator on the list.** EL Education is on Troy's stronger-alternatives shortlist.
- Sources: [Skillman Foundation](https://www.skillman.org/blog/standardswork-receives-grant-to-support-implementation-of-transformative-literacy-curriculum-in-detroit-public-schools/); [EL Education press](https://eleducation.org/news-and-events/press-releases/el-education-partners-with-detroit-public-school-to-implement-k-5-literacy-curriculum/); [DPSCD ELA page](https://www.detroitk12.org/academics/core-curriculum/english-language-arts-and-literacy).

**2. Marion County, KY — Chris Brady**
- 2,937 students; 61% econ-disadvantaged; 83% White, 11% Hispanic.
- **K-5 ELA: Amplify CKLA** K-5 + Amplify ELA 6-8. **LETRS** training for 44 teachers/administrators since 2022.
- "Grades 3-7 improved by over 10% in % proficient or above in reading" 2021-22 to 2024-25.
- **Direct relevance to Troy:** Demonstrates Amplify CKLA + LETRS in a small district.
- Source: [Case study PDF](https://educationscorecard.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/KY_Marion-County_Case-Study.pdf).

**3. Baltimore City Public Schools, MD — Sonja Santelises**
- 76,807 students; 70% econ-disadvantaged; 70% Black, 20% Hispanic.
- **K-5 ELA: Wit & Wisdom (Great Minds)** K-8 since 2018-19. DIBELS screener 3x/year for 10+ years.
- "Outpaced the state in growth in ELA" over 9 years. Chalkbeat: "lost far less ground in reading than schools with similar levels of poverty" during pandemic.
- **Direct relevance to Troy:** Largest urban Wit & Wisdom proof-point.
- Sources: [Knowledge Matters Campaign profile](https://knowledgematterscampaign.org/blog/baltimore-city-public-schools/); [BCPS reading readiness page](https://www.baltimorecityschools.org/page/reading-readiness-and-elementary-literacy); [Case study PDF](https://educationscorecard.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/MD_Baltimore-City-Public-Schools-Case-Study.pdf).

**4. Fond du Lac SD, WI — Matt Steinbarth**
- 6,457 students; 49.5% econ-disadvantaged; 59% White, 21% Hispanic, 9% Black.
- **K-5 ELA: Amplify CKLA** K-5 + Amplify ELA 6-8 + Bridges in Mathematics K-5.
- **Highest positive change in reading growth statewide in WI** from 2022 to 2024; jumped from 375th to 175th in state rankings. Reading was the breakthrough subject.
- **Direct relevance to Troy:** **Single strongest "demographically comparable + Amplify CKLA + numeric ELA win"** combo on the list. Mid-size, mixed-demographic WI district — translates directly.
- Source: [Case study PDF](https://educationscorecard.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/WI_Fond-du-Lac_Case-Study.pdf).

**5. Pierre SD, SD — Kelly Glodt**
- 2,680 students; 31% econ-disadvantaged; 67% White, 28% American Indian.
- **K-5 ELA: Open Court Reading (McGraw-Hill)** + Amplify 6-8. Foundational: **Orton-Gillingham, Heggerty, Barton** for intervention. Acadience screening.
- Case study uses the phrase "led a multi-year transition from a balanced literacy curriculum to a science of reading approach" — **most explicit narrated transition in the entire list**.
- **Direct relevance to Troy:** Open Court is older/knowledge-light — not on Troy's shortlist — but the *transition story* and intervention layer are relevant.
- Source: [Case study PDF](https://educationscorecard.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/SD_-Pierre-School-District-Case-Study.pdf).

### Tier 2 — Clear SoR Shift, Curriculum Different from Troy's Choices

**6. Atlanta Public Schools, GA — Bryan Johnson** (49,878; 60% econ-dis; 70% Black, 16% White, 9% Hispanic)
- Mixed **Benchmark Advance + HMH Into Reading**. Rollins Center for Language & Literacy PD. Amira ISIP screener added 2025-26. Phase 2 SoR adoption underway.
- **Only large urban district to show significant 4th-grade NAEP reading + math gain in 2024**; **+7 points NAEP 4th-grade reading basic+** 2022-2024.
- Sources: [APS Phase 2](https://www.atlantapublicschools.us/Page/73277); [AJC coverage](https://www.ajc.com/education/atlanta-tops-the-nation-with-major-4th-grade-reading-gains-this-program-could-be-why/ZSOMOJXOPVDRZC6X4B7ASCGZUE/).

**7. East Hartford, CT — Thomas Anderson** (6,504; 61% econ-dis; 45% White, 32% Hispanic)
- **Fundamentals Unlimited + Wilson Fundations 2nd ed + Heggerty Phonemic Awareness**.
- Two schools named CT "Schools of Distinction" (top 10%); district-wide ELA gains 2024-25.
- Source: [CT SDE approved K-3 list](https://portal.ct.gov/sde/academic-office/center-for-literacy-research-and-reading-success/information-about-districts-and-charter-schools/approved-k-3-reading-curriculum-models-or-programs-by-district).

**8. School City of East Chicago, IN — Stephen Bournés** (3,253; 69% econ-dis; 54% Hispanic, 43% Black)
- "Explicit, systematic phonics instruction + **Heggerty + Reading Horizons** intervention labs"; Acadience screener; SoR coaches.

**9. Starkville-Oktibbeha, MS — Tony McGee** (4,882; 67% econ-dis; 65% Black, 29% White)
- HQIM in ELA (not named in PDF); MS state SoR mandate. "Transitioned from balanced literacy to a structured literacy approach."

### Tier 3 — ELA Strategy Present, Specifics Unverified
- **Johnson City TN** (7,623; 36% econ-dis; 67% White) — TN HQIM mandate; specific core not named.
- **Spring Branch TX** (32,668; 55% econ-dis; 56% Hispanic, 7% Asian, 37% Emergent Bilingual) — TX STR framework; core not named.
- **Brandywine DE** (10,070; 25% econ-dis — Troy-like affluence) — SoR K-2 phonics; core not named.
- **Dover NH** (3,644; 27% econ-dis; 80% White, 6% Asian — most Troy-like demographics) — "shifted toward knowledge-building approaches"; core not named.
- **College Community IA** (5,800; 40% econ-dis; 65% White) — MTSS focus; no named ELA core.
- **Kuna Joint ID** (5,649; 38% low-income; 76% White) — moving to **HMH Into Reading** 2026-27; SoR PD since 2009.
- **Sikeston R-6 MO** (3,225; 70% econ-dis) — teacher-developed; state LETRS via "Read, Lead, Exceed."
- **Wayne County NC** (16,720; 59% econ-dis; 35% Black, 31% Hispanic, 29% White; 25% EL) — NC Standard Course of Study; reportedly still balanced literacy with leveled readers; **only district in cohort without clear SoR shift**.

## (4) Bottom Line for Troy

### Curriculum frequency among the 20 outperformers (verified named cores)

| Core curriculum | # of outperformer districts using it |
|---|---|
| **Wit & Wisdom (Great Minds)** | **3** (Baltimore, West Baton Rouge, Roanoke County) |
| **Amplify CKLA** | **2** (Marion County, Fond du Lac) |
| **EL Education** | **1** (Detroit) |
| **HMH Into Reading** | **2** (Atlanta mixed; Kuna 2026-27) |
| **Benchmark Advance** | **2** (Modesto, Atlanta mixed) |
| Open Court | 1 (Pierre) |
| Fundamentals Unlimited | 1 (East Hartford) |
| Teacher-developed | 1 (Sikeston) |
| **Collaborative Literacy** | **0** |
| Unverified | 7 |

### Foundational/phonics frequency
- **Heggerty:** 4 verified (Roanoke County, East Hartford, East Chicago, Pierre).
- **Wilson Fundations:** 2 verified (West Baton Rouge, East Hartford).
- **UFLI:** 1 verified (Modesto, *patched in* to fix Benchmark gaps).
- **Really Great Reading:** 1 (Roanoke County).
- **OG / Barton:** 1 (Pierre, intervention).
- **LETRS PD:** 3 explicit. **Every district that names a PD program names LETRS.**

### Key conclusions

1. **Zero of 20 outperformer districts adopted Collaborative Literacy as their K-5 core.** If Troy proceeds with Collab Lit + UFLI, it will be the *only district in either Education Scorecard cohort* doing so.
2. **The three most-common named cores in the outperformer group — Wit & Wisdom (3), Amplify CKLA (2), HMH Into Reading (2) — are all on Troy's "stronger alternatives" list.**
3. **Largest publicly-quantified Reading-Only gain is Modesto +13 weeks**, but their own leaders said the core (Benchmark Advance) was deficient and required a UFLI patch. **Driver was LETRS teacher knowledge.**
4. **Most Troy-comparable outperformer is Roanoke County VA** — affluent, ~70% White, suburban county. **Their stack is Heggerty + Really Great Reading + Wit & Wisdom — not a workshop core.**
5. **Fond du Lac WI** (highest reading growth statewide) uses **Amplify CKLA K-5 + DDI weekly principal-led data meetings** — the cleanest reproducible playbook for a mid-size district pursuing reading gains.
6. **EL Education works in Michigan** — Detroit DPSCD is the in-state proof point.
7. **UFLI as standalone has a single proof-point (Modesto) and was a remedial addition.** No outperformer pairs UFLI with a workshop-style core.

### Single-sentence summary

Weighting by (a) named verifiable curriculum, (b) magnitude of demographic-adjusted ELA gain, and (c) demographic comparability to Troy: the strongest signals are **Wit & Wisdom + Fundations** (West Baton Rouge / Baltimore / Roanoke County) and **Amplify CKLA + LETRS** (Fond du Lac / Marion County), with **EL Education (Detroit)** as the in-state Michigan validator. None of the 20 outperformer districts uses Collaborative Literacy as a K-5 core.
