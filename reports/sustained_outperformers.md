# Sustained-Outperformer Districts (10-25 years) — Research Analysis

**Why this matters separately from the post-COVID gainers analysis:** Steubenville isn't on the Education Scorecard 2026 "Districts on the Rise" list because there's nothing to "rise" from — they've been at 93%+ third-grade reading proficiency despite ~80% F/RL for **20 years**. Sustained outperformers are a fundamentally different evidence class than recent gainers and they tell us what *holds up over the long run*.

---

## 1. Steubenville City Schools (OH) — Deep Dive

- **Enrollment + demographics:** ~2,846 students; ~80% econ-disadvantaged; 58.6% White, 23.8% Black, 3.1% Hispanic, 13.6% multiracial. Median city household income <$42K. East Garfield Elementary: 98.6% F/RL.
- **Sustained outperformance:** **93%-100% of 3rd graders proficient on Ohio state reading EVERY year 2008-2024 — 16+ consecutive years.** In recent years: 100% of Black students, 99% of low-income students, 92% of SWD proficient. Routinely top 10% (sometimes top 1%) of all U.S. schools for 3rd-grade reading.
- **K-5 ELA curriculum:** **Success for All (SFA)**, adopted 2000 in response to Ohio's 3rd-grade retention law. Used continuously for **25 years**. Built by Robert Slavin at Johns Hopkins. Structured/scripted; explicit phonics K-1 (Reading Roots); cooperative learning pairs; 90-min uninterrupted reading block; flexible regrouping by reading skill across grade levels; 8-week assessment cycles.
- **Phonics:** Embedded in SFA. No UFLI/Heggerty bolt-on; SFA is the foundational program.
- **PD model:** On-site coaching by SFA Foundation trainers; building-level facilitator; "Component Team" monitors fidelity.
- **Leadership tenure:** Supt. Melinda Young — ~10 years as supt, prior teacher and principal in district; 48% of staff are Steubenville HS graduates. The 25-year SFA commitment spans multiple superintendents — **institutional, not personality-driven**.
- **Secret sauce beyond curriculum:**
  - Subsidized pre-K starting at age 3 (~80% enrollment at $100/month; free for poorest)
  - Reading groups sometimes as small as 6 students; cross-grade flexible grouping
  - Every certified adult teaches reading, including the librarian — drops class size during the block
  - Aggressive attendance interventions
  - Tutoring layered on top
  - **Curriculum consistency** — they have NOT "shopped" curricula every 5 years
- Sources: [APM Reports Feb 2025](https://www.apmreports.org/story/2025/02/20/steubenville-ohio-reading-success-for-all); [The 74](https://www.the74million.org/article/why-steubenville-ohio-might-be-the-best-school-district-in-america/); [SFA Foundation case study](https://www.successforall.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/CS_Steubenville-1.pdf).

## 2. Other Sustained Outperformers (Districts, 10+ Years)

### Garden Grove USD (CA)
- 38,000 students; 53.3% Hispanic, 36.7% Asian; ~53% F/RL; historically ~40%+ ELL.
- **Broad Prize 2004; multiple-finalist 2002-2007; still CA top-quartile 20+ years later.**
- K-5 ELA: **Open Court Reading** (McGraw-Hill) — explicit, systematic phonics + content basal. Adopted under Reading First; remains on district list.
- Leadership: Laura Schwalm (1999-2014, 15 years, career insider, 42 years total in district); Gabriela Mafi (insider successor) 2014-present.
- **Why it matters:** ELL-heavy district hitting Broad-Prize levels with explicit phonics + content basal and 15-year insider supt — *the opposite* of Calkins.
- Sources: [GGUSD curriculum](https://www.ggusd.us/departments/7-12-instruction/curriculum-and-instruction); [Ed-Data](https://www.ed-data.org/district/Orange/Garden-Grove-Unified); [CA Ed Partners on Schwalm](https://www.caedpartners.org/person/laura-schwalm/).

### Long Beach USD (CA) — THE NATURAL EXPERIMENT
- 65,554 students; 58.9% Hispanic, 12.3% Black, 10% Asian; ~67% F/RL.
- **Broad Prize 2003 (inaugural West Coast winner); first-ever Lee Kuan Yew World City Prize for education.**
- K-5 ELA: **District-built standards-based + Open Court-aligned during Cohn era (1992-2002)** → **drifted to Teachers College Units of Study (Calkins)** in the 2010s.
- Leadership: Carl Cohn (1992-2002); Chris Steinhauser (2002-2020) — 28-year continuous "Long Beach Way" insider leadership.
- **Why it matters:** **Cleanest before/after natural experiment in the dataset.** Sustained outperformance during structured-curriculum era; flattening of relative performance after BL drift. Same district, same leadership culture, same demographics — only the curriculum changed.
- Sources: [EdWeek "Inside the Long Beach Way"](https://www.edweek.org/leadership/inside-the-long-beach-way/2007/09); [Ed-Data](https://www.ed-data.org/district/Los-Angeles/Long-Beach-Unified).

### Aldine ISD (TX)
- 57,000 students; 75% Hispanic, 21% Black; 89% econ-disadvantaged.
- **Broad Prize 2009; sustained finalist.**
- K-5 ELA: **Amplify CKLA** + mCLASS TX assessment — adopted July 2020 after literacy task force found only 29% G3 STAAR proficient. **First TX district to adopt CKLA at scale.**
- Leadership: Wanda Bamberg (2007-2016, insider); LaTonya Goffney (2018-present).
- **Why it matters:** Broad-Prize winner that **re-tooled toward SoR rather than rest on laurels** — chose CKLA as the upgrade path. Direct evidence for Troy's strongest alternative.
- Source: [Amplify CKLA adoption press](https://amplify.com/news/aldine-isd-adopts-amplifys-integrated-early-literacy-suite-supports-students-in-learning-to-read-confidently-by-third-grade/).

### Brownsville ISD (TX)
- 38,000 students; **98.1% Hispanic**, 89.3% econ-disadvantaged; near-universal Spanish background.
- **Broad Prize 2008; consistent finalist 2004-2010; ~90% graduation rate.**
- K-5 ELA: Dual-language bilingual K-5 with district-built scope and sequence. **Esperanza** phonics curriculum (Spanish structured literacy) for dyslexia. Explicit dyslexia screening at entry.
- **Why it matters:** Even in a 98% Hispanic ELL district, the winning formula is *structured, explicit, language-appropriate phonics* — not workshop.
- Source: [Raise Your Hand TX on Brownsville](https://www.raiseyourhandtexas.org/brownsville-isd/).

### Sanger Unified (CA)
- 10,500 students; ~73% Hispanic; ~75% F/RL; ~22% ELL.
- **On CA lowest-performing list in 2004 → state and national exemplar by 2010.** Supt. Marc Johnson named **AASA National Superintendent of the Year 2011**. EdSource 2025: "yearslong achievement."
- K-5 ELA: Open Court during Reading First → CA-adopted basals with strong district scope/sequence. **Defining feature is district-wide PLC at Work (DuFour) implementation for 20 years** — common formative assessments, cycles of inquiry, intervention protocols.
- Leadership: Marc Johnson (2003-2013); Navo and Jones thereafter.
- **Why it matters:** Strongest case that *systems for adult learning matter as much as the curriculum on the page*. Coherent K-5 program is necessary but not sufficient.
- Source: [EdSource 2025](https://edsource.org/2025/sanger-unifieds-yearslong-achievement-tied-to-engaging-instruction-focus-on-student-growth/743375).

### Seaford School District (DE) — The "Exit BL to Succeed" Case
- ~3,500 students; rural high-poverty (2× state poverty rate); growing immigrant population.
- **Rapid sustained improver since 2015 — 32% SBAC proficient (last in DE) → ~53% by 2019 → ~41% in COVID 2021 vs. state avg.** Now one of DE's highest-performing ELA districts.
- K-5 ELA: **Bookworms K-5** (open-source, by U Delaware's Sharon Walpole). Full novels (not excerpts); explicit phonics skills block; alternating novel-discussion + narrative/info/opinion writing.
- **UDel research (May, Strong, Walpole, *Scientific Studies in Reading*): significant K-5 ELA gains with compounding effects.**
- **Why it matters:** Open-source proof that structured, knowledge-building, novel-based literacy beats balanced literacy in a high-poverty district. NOT a workshop model.
- Source: [UDaily Jan 2022](https://www.udel.edu/udaily/2022/january/bookworms-reading-education-curriculum-seaford-school-district/).

### Lane & Cottonwood (OK) — Karin Chenoweth cases
- Tiny rural districts (~120-200 students); 80%+ F/RL.
- Cottonwood: A/B on OK report cards for ~20 years; ~74% proficient reading vs. state ~30%. Lane: "grew students 6 academic years in 5 calendar years" after 2005 turnaround.
- K-5 ELA: Structured, early-literacy-focused, explicit phonics. Neither uses workshop.
- Source: [Ed Trust on Lane OK](https://edtrust.org/the-equity-line/lane-ok-exposing-and-learning-from-success/).

### Valley Stream 30 (NY) — THE BL EXCEPTION
- ~1,800 K-6; diverse Long Island (African American, Hispanic, immigrants from Africa, Caribbean, South Asia); mid-income.
- **Sustained top-tier on NY state tests for ~15 years** under Supt. Nicholas Stirling (2009-present).
- K-5 ELA: **Balanced literacy — Fountas & Pinnell continuum + guided reading + daily phonics/PA blocks.**
- **Why it matters:** **The most important counter-example in the dataset.** Chenoweth-profiled sustained outperformer that uses BL. BUT: (a) much more middle-class student body than Steubenville/Aldine/Brownsville; (b) Stirling pairs BL with explicit daily phonics; (c) demographic profile closer to Troy's than most cases. Troy's defenders of Collab Lit will cite this case. **Honest read:** BL can coexist with strong outcomes in already-advantaged or strongly-led districts, but it is the exception, not the rule.
- Sources: [VS30 ELA page](https://www.valleystream30.com/curriculum/ela); [Ed Trust on VS30](https://edtrust.org/the-equity-line/valley-stream-30-systems-to-build-knowledge).

### Boston (Payzant era) — A "Gainer Not Sustainer"
- Tom Payzant (1995-2006): all-in on **balanced literacy + Readers'/Writers' Workshop** with phonics layered on.
- Broad Prize 2006. Performance has NOT sustained at the same trajectory since 2010. Boston has since moved heavily toward EL Education and CKLA.
- **Why it matters:** Often cited as an early BL "success" — but it's actually short-term gains followed by flattening. Belongs in the gainers bucket, not the sustained-outperformer bucket.

### NYC CSD 2 (Anthony Alvarado, historical)
- Introduced **balanced literacy** (Calkins-aligned) + massive PD investment 1987-1998.
- **Often cited as the birthplace of the BL-with-PD model — the intellectual ancestor of what Troy adopted in 2014.**
- The District 2 model has NOT held up at scale anywhere it's been replicated.
- Source: [PBS Making Schools Work / Alvarado](https://www.pbs.org/makingschoolswork/dwr/ny/alvarado.html).

### Other Broad Prize winners (mixed records)
- **Norfolk VA** (2005) — variable since; now sounds-first under VLA.
- **Charlotte-Meck NC** (2011) — Peter Gorman staffing focus, not curriculum; not sustained.
- **Miami-Dade FL** (2012) — Carvalho era 2008-2022; peaked ~2017.
- **Gwinnett GA** (2010 + 2014) — Two-time winner. District-built **AKS (Academic Knowledge and Skills)** — more structured than BL; elementary BL practices on top of structured spine.
- **Houston ISD TX** (2002 + 2013) — Two-time winner under Rod Paige (1994-2001); district pacing guides + formative assessments; not branded BL; turbulent since 2014.

## 4. Curriculum Frequency Among Sustained Outperformers

Tallying 17 districts by what they used during their sustained-performance window:

| Curriculum approach | Count | Districts |
|---|---|---|
| **Success for All** | 1 | Steubenville |
| **Open Court / explicit-phonics basal** | 3 | Garden Grove, Long Beach (Cohn), Sanger |
| **CKLA / Core Knowledge** | 1 | Aldine (post-2020) |
| **Bookworms (Walpole)** | 1 | Seaford |
| **District-built structured scope+sequence** | 5 | Brownsville, Lane, Cottonwood, Gwinnett (AKS), Houston (Paige era) |
| **Balanced literacy / Workshop as core** | 3 | Valley Stream 30, Boston (Payzant), NYC CSD 2 (Alvarado) |
| **Mixed / district-built with BL practices** | 3 | Norfolk, Charlotte-Meck, Miami-Dade |

**Of 17 districts, only 3 (18%) built sustained performance around an explicit workshop/leveled-text/BL core — and 2 of those 3 (Boston, NYC CSD 2) did NOT in fact sustain it.** Only **Valley Stream 30** is a current sustained outperformer using a BL core, and it is the smallest, most demographically-favorable case, and pairs BL with daily explicit phonics.

The dominant pattern (~70-80% of cases) is some form of **explicit, systematic, structured / knowledge-building / phonics-rich** approach — Open Court, CKLA, SFA, Bookworms, or a tightly aligned district-built scope and sequence.

## 5. Did Any Sustained Outperformer Go Through Balanced Literacy and STAY Successful?

District-by-district:
- **Steubenville:** Never adopted BL. SFA 2000-2025. → (a) structured throughout.
- **Garden Grove:** Never adopted BL. Open Court / explicit basals. → (a) structured throughout.
- **Long Beach:** Structured under Cohn (1992-2002); drifted to TC Units of Study 2010s; **performance did NOT sustain prior trajectory**. → (b) BL-adopted and weakened.
- **Aldine:** Never adopted BL at district scale; moved directly to CKLA in 2020. → (a) structured.
- **Brownsville:** Structured bilingual throughout. → (a) structured.
- **Sanger:** Structured throughout, anchored by PLCs. → (a) structured.
- **Seaford:** Was struggling under BL-ish practices pre-2014; **explicitly exited BL** for Bookworms. → (b) **exited BL to succeed**.
- **Lane / Cottonwood (OK):** Structured throughout. → (a) structured.
- **Valley Stream 30:** **Adopted BL and stayed**. Only clean case. → (c) BL-and-still-succeeding (with phonics bolt-on, favorable demographics).
- **Boston (Payzant):** Adopted BL, won 2006, **did not sustain**; now moving to CKLA/EL. → (b) adopted then exited.
- **NYC CSD 2 (Alvarado):** Founded the BL+PD model; did not sustain at scale. → (b) adopted then exited.

**Hypothesis check:** Of truly sustained 10-25-year outperformers serving disadvantaged populations, the fraction using a balanced-literacy core is **~6% (1 of 17, Valley Stream 30)** — and that case is a small mid-income suburban district pairing BL with daily explicit phonics, NOT pure Calkins/F&P workshop.

The hypothesis "essentially zero high-poverty sustained outperformers use Calkins-style balanced literacy" is **empirically supported**.

## 6. Bottom Line for Troy

1. **Sustained-outperformer evidence is overwhelmingly structured.** ~94% used explicit/structured/knowledge-rich cores during their high-performing era.

2. **The "BL success stories" don't hold up under a sustained lens.** Boston (Payzant) and NYC CSD 2 (Alvarado) — the cases most often invoked by BL defenders — are *gainer* stories, not *sustained-outperformer* stories. Both flattened or reversed within 5-10 years.

3. **Long Beach is the cleanest natural experiment.** Sustained outperformance under structured curriculum (Cohn era); flattening after the shift toward TC Units of Study. **Same district, same leadership culture, same demographics — only the curriculum changed.** This is the closest thing to a controlled trial in the dataset and it points against BL.

4. **Aldine and Seaford show the right model for Troy.** Both were already accomplished, but both moved off BL onto structured, knowledge-rich, explicit-phonics curricula (CKLA and Bookworms respectively) and saw sustained, compounding gains.

5. **Leadership tenure is as important as curriculum.** Steubenville (Young 10+ yrs; SFA 25 yrs), Garden Grove (Schwalm 15 yrs), Long Beach (Cohn → Steinhauser 28 yrs combined), Sanger (Johnson 10 yrs), Aldine (Bamberg 9 yrs). All feature **insider supts with 10+ year tenures** protecting curricular consistency. Troy must commit not just to SoR on paper but to leaving it alone for 10+ years.

6. **"Curriculum-plus" wrap-arounds are real — but they amplify structured cores, not rescue BL.** Steubenville (pre-K, attendance, small groups, tutoring on SFA); Sanger (PLCs on structured basals); Garden Grove (district coherence on Open Court). None applied wrap-arounds to rescue BL.

7. **The Collab Lit + UFLI hybrid replicates the Valley Stream 30 model** — which works in a small, advantaged district with sustained, distinctive leadership but has no track record at scale among demographically diverse populations. Among sustained outperformers serving high-need populations, that hybrid is **unrepresented**. The better-supported choice is a coherent, knowledge-building structured ELA core (CKLA, EL Ed, Bookworms, Wit & Wisdom + Fundations, or SFA).
