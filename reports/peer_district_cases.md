# Peer District Cases: K-5 ELA Shifts from Balanced Literacy to the Science of Reading

## Executive summary

Suburban districts that look demographically like Troy — large Asian-American enrollment, high household incomes, US-News-ranked high schools, ~30%+ ELL populations — are roughly two to three years into the same shift Troy is now contemplating. The Pacific Northwest (Bellevue, Issaquah, Lake Washington) has moved fastest; the California Peninsula (Palo Alto) has the cleanest published evidence of impact; New Jersey and Texas are mid-stream; Michigan peers are still defining what "approved" means under MDE's new literacy lists. High-poverty outliers — Steubenville, Bethlehem Area SD, Mississippi, Louisiana, Tennessee — provide the long-run "what's possible" picture. Berkeley/Virginia/NYC Phase 2 supply the cautionary tales.

Headline finding for Troy: districts that paired a **knowledge-building core** (CKLA / EL Education / ARC Core / Wit & Wisdom / Benchmark Advance) with a **structured foundational-skills program** (UFLI, SIPPS, Heggerty, 95 Phonics, Really Great Reading) AND invested in deep teacher training (LETRS-equivalent) showed the strongest outcomes. Districts that adopted only one of those three legs under-performed.

## BUCKET 1 — Demographically similar peer districts

### Bellevue School District (WA)
- **Enrollment & demographics:** ~19,855 students, 30 schools; **44.3% Asian**, 25.3% white, 15.1% Hispanic/Latino, 4.1% Black; minority enrollment 70%. #1 in WA for Asian enrollment.
- **Prior curriculum:** Locally-developed balanced-literacy framework with Lucy Calkins / Reading Workshop influence.
- **Shift:** P-5 Literacy Adoption Committee piloted **American Reading Company (ARC) Core** and **Benchmark Advance / Adelante** during 2023-24. Selected **ARC Core**; 2024-25 strategic plan lists "ARC Literacy Curriculum Adoption Implementation Year 2."
- **Outcomes:** Year 2 in progress 2025-26; pre-shift 3rd-grade ELA already among highest in WA but with persistent gaps for Hispanic, low-income, SPED students.
- **Sources:** https://bsd405.org/departments/teaching-and-learning/curriculum-adoption/current-curriculum-adoption-committees/p-5-literacy-instructional-materials-adoption-committee/ ; https://strategicplan.bsd405.org/2024-2025-annual-plan ; https://www.usnews.com/education/k12/washington/districts/bellevue-school-district-109251

### Lake Washington School District (WA)
- **Enrollment & demographics:** ~30,882 students, 57 schools (Redmond/Kirkland/Sammamish, Microsoft-adjacent); **39.4% Asian**, 37.9% white, 11.1% Hispanic/Latino. Extremely close demographic match to Troy.
- **Prior curriculum:** Locally-developed K-5 ELA aligned to balanced literacy / Reader's & Writer's Workshop, last refreshed 2018.
- **Shift (in progress):** Reading Advisory Committee using **The Reading League** criteria picked two K-5 ELA curricula to pilot Sept 2025–March 2026. Final recommendation to board **spring 2026**. Leads: Kelly Moses Pease, Whitney Blackstone.
- **Outcomes:** TBD — LWSD is roughly one calendar year ahead of where Troy will be in deliberations. **The single most useful real-time peer for Troy to track.**
- **Sources:** https://www.lwsd.org/services/curriculum-instruction/curriculum-review-adoption ; https://www.lwsd.org/services/communications/connections-e-newsletters/connections-october-9-2025

### Issaquah School District (WA)
- **Enrollment & demographics:** ~19,452 students, 30 schools; **35.8% Asian**, 40.5% white, 10.8% Hispanic/Latino; only **10.5% economically disadvantaged**.
- **Prior curriculum:** Words Their Way + locally-built ELA modules; balanced literacy framework.
- **Shift:** **March 28, 2024** — Comprehensive Literacy Adoption Committee voted 100% consensus to recommend **Benchmark Advance / Adelante** K-5 starting fall 2024. Teachers cited explicit SoR alignment. Words Their Way Classroom retained for above-standard students.
- **Outcomes:** One year of implementation; first post-adoption SBA cohort results pending summer 2025/fall 2026.
- **Sources:** https://www.isd411.org/academics/academics-overview/curriculum-selection-process/elementary-literacy-adoption ; https://www.isd411.org/about-us/announcements/single-announcement/~board/district-announcements/post/elementary-literacy-committee-recommends-benchmark-advance-curriculum

### Palo Alto Unified (CA)
- **Enrollment & demographics:** ~10,500 students; ~35% Asian, ~31% white, ~13% Hispanic/Latino; significant ELL population.
- **Prior curriculum:** **Lucy Calkins Units of Study** + **Fountas & Pinnell** running records — textbook balanced literacy stack.
- **Shift:** Launched **Every Student Reads Initiative (ESRI)** in 2021. Replaced Calkins Units of Study with a knowledge-building core + decodable books; mandated **Orton-Gillingham** training for ALL TK-3 teachers, reading specialists, and elementary principals; replaced F&P with **i-Ready** universal screener.
- **Outcomes (strong, publicly reported):**
  - Low-income Latino 3rd graders reading at/above grade level: **20% → 47%** in 2 years.
  - PAUSD named **CA's most-improved district** on California Reading Report Card; +9 points for low-income Latino students vs. statewide −8.
  - 2023 disaggregated CAASPP: Black +33 pp, ELL +37 pp, Hispanic/Latino +36 pp. In 2025, 82.26% of PAUSD students met/exceeded ELA standard, +1.74 pp from 2024.
- **Why this matters for Troy:** Cleanest published case of a high-Asian, high-achieving, ELL-significant suburban district leaving Calkins for SoR and narrowing the white-Asian/low-income gap without harming top-end performance.
- **Sources:** https://www.pausd.org/learning/esri ; https://edsource.org/2023/how-any-district-can-could-move-the-needle-on-early-literacy/697137 ; https://www.pausd.org/about-us/news/announcements/do/~board/a/post/significant-growth-for-historically-underserved-students ; https://www.paloaltoonline.com/news/2023/06/07/reading-initiative-is-improving-palo-alto-students-skills-tests-show/

### Plano ISD & Frisco ISD (TX)
- **Demographic similarity:** Frisco is **42.5% Asian**, 28.7% white, 12.4% Hispanic, 10.5% Black, ~9.8% econ-disadvantaged — closest national match to Troy's raw demographics. Plano: 21.5% Asian (2015 figure), 40.2% Anglo. Frisco enrolls ~66,700; Plano ~46,000.
- **Prior curriculum:** Both publicly described themselves as **"Balanced Literacy"** through 2022-23. Frisco's elementary instructional model still uses the term. Plano builds via Understanding by Design (UbD) around a balanced literacy core.
- **Shift:** Texas HB 1605 (2023) and TEA's HQIM/Bluebonnet review cycle are pushing both toward state-approved SoR-aligned cores. Likely options: **Amplify CKLA** (used in Aldine with 30%→50% gains), **HMH Into Reading**, TEA Bluebonnet. Neither Plano nor Frisco has publicly named a single replacement core.
- **Sources:** https://www.friscoisd.org/departments/elementary-literacy/instructional-model ; https://www.pisd.edu/Page/16620 ; https://texas2036.org/hqim/ ; https://tea.texas.gov/academics/instructional-materials/instructional-materials-certification-resource-list.pdf ; https://schools.texastribune.org/districts/frisco-isd/demographics/

### Michigan peers (Bloomfield Hills, Birmingham, Northville, Novi)
- **Status:** None has yet adopted from MDE's new state-approved literacy lists. MDE released initial approved lists **Dec 18, 2025**. MDE/MiLEAP released "Every Child Reads Next Steps Forward" **March 4, 2026**, with FY27 budget request of **$625M** for LETRS training and curriculum-adoption incentives.

- **Implication:** **Troy is at the front of the Michigan suburban peer pack, not behind it.**

### West Windsor-Plainsboro / Princeton / Millburn (NJ)

## BUCKET 2 — Outlier successes (high-poverty districts beating their demographics)

### Steubenville City Schools (OH) — the gold standard
- **Demographics:** ~2,200 students; median household income <$42K; **~80% F/RL**; majority white with significant Black enrollment; Appalachian post-industrial Ohio.
- **Curriculum:** **Success for All (SFA)** since 2000 — **25+ years** of district-wide commitment. SFA is whole-school reform developed at Johns Hopkins (Slavin/Madden): structured explicit phonics, cooperative learning, 90-minute reading block, frequent regrouping. **Fully aligned with Science of Reading.**
- **Outcomes:**
  - For ~20 years, **93%+ of students** proficient on Ohio state reading test.
  - **2024:** 100% of Black students, 99% of low-income students, 92% of SWD reached reading proficiency.
  - Wells Academy: 97% math, 100% reading proficient. Ed Trust Dispelling the Myth Award 2008; National Blue Ribbon 2003-05.
- **Per-pupil spending:** ~$10,718, **~$1,500 BELOW Ohio average**.
- **Key takeaway:** **20-year consistency + coherent program + stable leadership** (Supt. Melinda Young, decade+ tenure; 48% of staff are local grads).
- **Sources:** https://www.apmreports.org/story/2025/02/20/steubenville-ohio-reading-success-for-all ; https://ohiocapitaljournal.com/2025/04/02/why-steubenville-ohio-might-be-the-best-school-district-in-america/ ; https://www.the74million.org/article/why-steubenville-ohio-might-be-the-best-school-district-in-america/ ; https://www.successforall.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/CS_Steubenville-1.pdf ; https://www.the74million.org/article/sold-a-story-6-takeaways-from-deep-dive-into-literacy-in-steubenville-ohio/

### Bethlehem Area School District (PA) — early-adopter template
- **Demographics:** ~12,800 students (close to Troy's ~12,000); 37.7% white, 46.4% Hispanic, 11.2% Black, 2.8% Asian; **42.1% economically disadvantaged**.
- **Prior:** Lucy Calkins-influenced **balanced literacy**.
- **Shift:** Then-superintendent **Dr. Joseph Roy** (PA Supt of the Year 2017) and CAO **Jack Silva** found in 2015 that only **56%** of 3rd graders were proficient. Pivoted to structured literacy at K in 2015-16; invested **~$3M** in training, materials, coaching.
- **Outcomes:** **84% of K students** met/exceeded benchmarks by 2018; three elementary schools hit **100%**. The 2018 3rd-grade cohort (first K cohort under new instruction) saw "significant increase" in PSSA literacy. Featured in NPR's 2019 "Why Millions of Kids Can't Read" and APM's *Sold a Story*.
- **Key takeaway:** **Template case** — mid-size, demographically mixed; started early; invested in training first; reproducible 3rd-grade gains visible 3 years downstream of the K change.
- **Sources:** https://www.npr.org/2019/01/02/677722959/why-millions-of-kids-cant-read-and-what-better-teaching-can-do-about-it ; https://thebrownandwhite.com/2022/09/26/novel-programs-impact-bethlehem-youth-literacy/ ; https://thephiladelphiacitizen.org/better-way-teach-reading/

### Mississippi — the "Miracle"
- **Policy stack (post-2013):** Literacy-Based Promotion Act; mandatory **LETRS** for every K-3 teacher (~19,000 trained); universal K-3 screeners; literacy coaches; 3rd-grade retention gate.
- **Outcomes:** 4th-grade NAEP reading **50th (2013) → 9th nationally**. **#1 demographically adjusted in BOTH reading AND math in 2024.** Only state where 4th-grade reading **outscored national average** despite near-bottom family income.
- **Caveats:** Part of gain attributable to retention (retained 3rd graders not tested in 4th); even adjusting for selection bias, underlying gains real and demo-adjusted analyses confirm #1.
- **Key takeaway:** **Policy + training + accountability stack** produced results — not any one curriculum. LETRS is the binding agent.
- **Sources:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mississippi_Miracle ; https://www.the74million.org/article/there-really-was-a-mississippi-miracle-in-reading-states-should-learn-from-it/ ; https://www.mississippifirst.org/contextualizing-mississippis-2024-naep-scores/ ; https://www.chalkbeat.org/2023/7/18/23799124/mississippi-miracle-test-scores-naep-early-literacy-grade-retention-reading-phonics/ ; https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S027277572400092X

### Louisiana ELA Guidebooks
- **Curriculum:** State-built, open-source, knowledge-building **Louisiana ELA Guidebooks** (3-5 launched 2022, 6-8 in 2024) on Louisiana Curriculum Hub. Paired with statewide SoR teacher training.
- **Outcomes:** Louisiana 4th graders **42nd → 16th** nationally on NAEP reading 2022→2024 (26-spot, 2-year climb). One of only two states whose 4th graders surpassed pre-pandemic NAEP in BOTH reading and math (Mississippi the other). Gains reached econ-disadvantaged students and SWD, beating their national peers.
- **Key takeaway:** **State-built knowledge-building core + SoR foundational-skills training** is sufficient; you don't need to buy a vendor.
- **Sources:** https://www.edsurge.com/news/2025-02-25-louisiana-s-naep-score-a-victory-for-the-science-of-reading ; https://www.joannejacobs.com/post/getting-better-how-louisiana-is-raising-reading-and-math-scores ; https://doe.louisiana.gov/educators/instructional-support/ela-guidebooks ; https://edreports.org/resources/article/louisianas-education-model-a-student-improvement-blueprint

### Tennessee — Reading 360
- **Policy:** Reading 360 (since 2021): optional grants, **60 hours of SoR training** (30 online + 30 in-person cohort), state ELA HQIM, 3rd-grade retention gate.
- **Trained educators:** 30,000+ since 2021; 97% feel equipped to apply training.
- **Outcomes:** 3rd-grade ELA proficiency hit state's **highest-ever** in 2023 (40% proficient, +4.3 pp YoY, +8 pp from 2021 trough). 2025 TCAP: ~41% met/exceeded ELA; post-pandemic high in all four tested subjects.
- **Caveats:** 20-pt gap between econ-disadvantaged and non-disadvantaged persists.
- **Sources:** https://www.chalkbeat.org/tennessee/2025/07/09/tcap-reading-scores-released-statewide-third-grade-retention-law/ ; https://www.tn.gov/education/news/2023/5/22/tennessee-makes-historic-gains-in-third-grade-reading--offers-strong-support-for-students-.html ; https://tnscore.org/perspectives-and-press/perspectives/2025-tcap-data-our-compass-for-the-work-ahead ; https://www.tn.gov/education/reading-360.html

### Oakland Unified — Reading by 3rd Grade
- **Curriculum:** District-wide **SIPPS** in all K-2 elementaries; **EL Education** core in some schools; FluentSeeds 4-day SIPPS course for all K-2 literacy tutors.
- **Outcomes:** Mixed. 3rd-grade SBAC at-or-above grade level only ~36% in 2021-22; tutoring gains uneven, strongest in K, weaker by grade 2.
- **Key takeaway:** **Even an SoR-aligned foundational-skills program (SIPPS) is not enough** without the knowledge core, fidelity supports, and coherent K-5 stack. Foundational skills add-on is necessary but not sufficient.
- **Sources:** https://www.ousd.org/english-language-arts-ela/foundational-skills-curriculum-at-ousd ; https://www.kqed.org/news/11970992/oakland-unified-literacy-tutor-program-shows-promising-early-results-despite-challenges ; https://oaklandside.org/2022/11/21/literacy-tutoring-programs-show-promise-in-oakland/ ; https://edsource.org/2022/how-one-california-elementary-school-sees-success-after-overhauling-its-reading-program/668351

### New York City — NYC Reads
- **Policy:** Phase 1 (fall 2023) and Phase 2 (fall 2024) schools must use one of three: **HMH Into Reading**, **Wit & Wisdom**, **EL Education**. 22 of 32 local districts chose Into Reading; 5 EL Ed; 5 Wit & Wisdom. First-year training estimated **$35M**.
- **Outcomes:**
  - Grades 3-8 ELA proficiency on NY State assessments: **49.1% (2024) → 56.3% (2025), +7.2 pp**.
  - K-5 above-national-median screener: 41.6% spring 2025, +2.5 pp YoY.
  - **Phase 1 teachers 55% confident vs. Phase 2 teachers 38%** — stark fidelity gap between cohorts.
  - **District 11 (EL Education)** outperformed both other curricula AND non-pilot schools — natural experiment favoring EL Ed.
- **Sources:** https://www.chalkbeat.org/newyork/2025/07/07/nyc-reads-solves-screener-data-eric-adams-school-curriculum-mandate/ ; https://www.k12dive.com/news/teachers-new-york-city-reading-curriculum/739282/ ; https://www.chalkbeat.org/newyork/2024/09/06/what-to-know-about-nyc-reads-curriculum-mandate-for-schools/ ; https://www.edweek.org/teaching-learning/how-the-largest-school-district-is-adjusting-to-the-science-of-reading/2024/09 ; https://www.nyc.gov/mayors-office/news/2025/08/mayor-adams--chancellor-aviles-ramos-celebrate-grades-3-8-state-test-

## BUCKET 3 — Cautionary tales

### Berkeley Unified (CA) — the holdout
2017 dyslexia lawsuit; 2021 settlement; Berkeley **kept Lucy Calkins Units of Study** through 2024. January 2024 EdSource/Berkeleyside investigation documented continued three-cueing instruction years after it was discredited and banned in multiple states. Berkeley added a universal dyslexia screener and Tier-2 phonics intervention but did NOT replace the core. **Lesson:** Adopting a screener and adding intervention is NOT the same as fixing Tier 1. The most common form of stall.
**Sources:** https://edsource.org/2024/berkeley-schools-use-a-discredited-reading-curriculum-why-is-it-still-in-classrooms/704503 ; https://www.berkeleyside.org/2024/01/24/units-of-study-science-of-reading-berkeley-unified ; https://www.kqed.org/news/11973560/7-years-after-lawsuit-berkeley-schools-slow-to-adopt-effective-reading-curriculum

### Virginia Literacy Act — speed-and-scripting backlash
2022 VLA mandated K-3 (later K-8 ELA) align to SoR. VDOE produced an approved-program list with a hard cueing-detection criterion. **NCTE published a formal critique** ("Virginia's Cautionary Tale") arguing the compressed timeline forced districts into scripted, vendor-controlled curricula and de-professionalized teachers. Middle-school ELA teachers swept into LETRS-style PD without schedule, coaching, or prep. **Lesson:** Pace and supports matter. Troy has the advantage of being on its own timeline.
**Sources:** https://ncte.org/blog/2022/12/science-reading-state-policies/ ; https://www.doe.virginia.gov/teaching-learning-assessment/k-12-standards-instruction/english-reading-literacy/literacy/virginia-literacy-act ; https://dailyprogress.com/news/state-regional/education/virginia-literacy-act-richmond-public-schools-chimborazo/article_ab8f2c4a-b174-5b16-9c3d-b46420f98a1b.html

### NYC Reads — Phase 2 confidence gap
Phase 1 teacher confidence 55%; **Phase 2 confidence 38%** — 17-pt gap. EdWeek July 2025 and K-12 Dive Nov 2025 document teachers mixing old materials with new — **curriculum fidelity is the binding constraint**. **Lesson:** Even the best core fails without 2+ years of structured PD, sustained coaching, and a "stop teaching the old thing" policy.
**Sources:** https://www.k12dive.com/news/new-york-teachers-report-gaps-in-preparation-for-science-of-reading/756302/ ; https://www.edweek.org/teaching-learning/a-good-deal-of-nostalgia-new-yorks-uneven-embrace-of-the-science-of-reading/2025/07

### Massachusetts — Cambridge / Brookline / Amherst
2023 Boston Globe investigation: **>1/3 of MA districts** still used the Heinemann/Calkins/F&P stack, including Amherst, Brookline, Cambridge — wealthier suburbs not unlike Troy. DESE offered up to **$200K grants** to replace. Dec 2024 lawsuit by two MA mothers alleging deceptive marketing was dismissed May 2025 under "educational malpractice" doctrine, but accelerated affluent-district exits. **Lesson:** **Affluence is the single biggest risk factor for stalling**, because high aggregate scores let leadership defer change. Don't let Troy be in that group.
**Sources:** https://www.bostonglobe.com/2024/12/04/metro/science-of-reading-lawsuit-calkins-fountas-pinnell-heinemann/ ; https://www.apmreports.org/story/2025/05/23/judge-scuttles-lawsuit-over-massachusetts-reading-curriculum ; https://www.edweek.org/teaching-learning/4-things-to-know-about-the-literacy-lawsuit-targeting-lucy-calkins-and-fountas-pinnell/2024/12

### North Carolina — large-scale LETRS, modest NAEP movement
NC trained **44,000+ teachers** in K-5 LETRS by June 2024. NAEP 2024 showed **no significant change** since 2022; reading remained below national average even as math moved above it. DIBELS 8 showed early-grades progress, but NAEP timing (Q1 2024) caught the cohort too early. **Lesson:** **2-3 year lag** between training rollout and tested-cohort gains. Don't declare failure after Year 1. **Plan board communications and outcome reviews on a 5-year horizon.**
**Sources:** https://www.ednc.org/nc-students-score-at-or-above-average-on-national-assessment-but-pandemic-gaps-persist/ ; https://www.dpi.nc.gov/news/press-releases/2024/04/03/middle-year-reading-assessment-shows-continued-growth-early-grades-students ; https://www.wfae.org/education/2022-11-17/latest-reading-scores-leave-room-for-debate-on-ncs-investment-in-science-of-reading

## Synthesis — what these cases tell Troy

1. **The shift works when it's a stack, not a single product.** Palo Alto, Bethlehem, Steubenville, Mississippi, Louisiana all paired (a) knowledge-building/structured core, (b) explicit systematic phonics, (c) deep teacher training (Orton-Gillingham / LETRS / 60+ hours), and (d) universal screening. Troy's tentative Collaborative Literacy + UFLI direction fits this stack pattern; the risk is execution.
2. **Training depth predicts results more than curriculum choice.** Mississippi and Tennessee chose policy + LETRS-level training over any single curriculum and got the strongest NAEP movement. NYC Phase 2 vs. Phase 1 (17-pt confidence gap) shows what under-training looks like. **Budget 60+ hours of training per K-5 teacher across years 1-2, with sustained coaching in year 3+.**
3. **Expect a 2-3 year lag.** NC LETRS 2021 → NAEP 2024 flat. Bethlehem K change 2015 → 3rd-grade bounce 2018. **Plan board comms on a 5-year horizon.**
4. **The biggest threat is not picking a bad curriculum — it's picking a good one and stalling.** Berkeley, Cambridge, Brookline, Amherst show how affluent districts use high baseline scores to defer change. Troy's white-Asian 3rd-grade gap is the single best argument against the "demographics are doing the work" defense.
