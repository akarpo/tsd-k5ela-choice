# Troy School District — Data Brief

Source: `tsd-achievement.karpowitsch.org` master workbook (`G3-G7_Achievement_Data.xlsx`, retrieved May 2026). M-STEP "Proficient or Advanced." Within-district trends are valid; cross-state absolute % are not.

## 1. What Troy is currently teaching

| Subject | Program | Adopted |
|---|---|---|
| K-5 ELA | **Units of Study (Lucy Calkins / Heinemann TCRWP)** | **Fall 2014** |
| 6-8 ELA | Units of Study Writing + balanced literacy | ~2014 |
| K-5 Math | Bridges in Mathematics | 2023 |
| 6-8 Math | Imagine IM (Illustrative Math) | May 2023 |

Troy's current K-5 ELA core is **Units of Study by Lucy Calkins** — the program that is the central villain of APM Reports' "Sold a Story" series and the most-publicly-criticized balanced-literacy curriculum in the country. Heinemann/TCRWP severed the relationship with Calkins' Teachers College Reading and Writing Project in 2023 after the SoR critique reached crisis levels. Calkins herself published a revised "Units of Study" with phonics added in 2022, but EdReports still rates the program "Does Not Meet Expectations" on Foundational Skills.

**This is the single most important fact:** Troy is not "shifting from balanced literacy" — Troy is **11 years into Lucy Calkins specifically**.

### What jumps out
- **Six years post-pre-COVID and still 6.1 points below baseline overall.** The 2024 trough (63.2%) was Troy's worst in the dataset; 2025 recovered some but not all.
- **The Asian subgroup is in free-fall (-7.4 pts).** This is the largest Asian-subgroup decline of any peer district in the dataset (see §4). The user's framing — "Asian 3G ELA scores still outperform whites" — is correct *in absolute terms*, but the **Asian cohort itself has lost 7.4 points** since 2019. The ESL narrative is doing a lot of work that the data doesn't support.
- **White students bottomed at 59.5% in 2024.** For an affluent suburb, this is the most damning number on the page. White students in Troy underperform the Asian subgroup in every year shown, often by 15-20 points — this is **not** a demographic story, it is a curriculum/instruction story.
- **SWD subgroup collapsed -10.4 points.** Students with disabilities are the most sensitive to instructional method; a -10.4 pt drop is the strongest curriculum signal in the table. SoR research (Foorman, Torgesen, Kilpatrick) is unambiguous that systematic phonics matters most for this group.
- **English Learners -8.9 pts** despite EL enrollment growing. This is the subgroup Troy publicly cites; but the *Non-EL* group dropped -5.4 pts too. EL composition is not the cause.

**Headline:** Of Troy's eight self-selected peer districts, **only Bellevue has formally adopted an SoR-aligned program**, and they did it in 2024-25 — too recent to show outcomes yet. Most "high-performing" suburban peers nationally are still in balanced literacy. This means Troy is choosing a path that few of its self-identified peers have completed; the better case-study peers will come from outside this 8-district group (Steubenville, Bethlehem PA, Mississippi/Louisiana state initiatives, NYC Reads — handed off to the peer-district research agent).

## 5. The picture this paints for curriculum selection

1. **The status-quo program is the worst-rated comprehensive K-5 ELA on the U.S. market.** Units of Study (Calkins) is rated *Does Not Meet Expectations* by EdReports across all gateways and is the named driver of "Sold a Story."
2. **The trajectory is not a COVID story.** Six years out, peers with similar (or weaker) shifts have recovered or grown. Troy hasn't. The instructional core is the most parsimonious explanation.
3. **The proposed replacement (Collaborative Literacy + UFLI Foundations) is one of the weakest SoR transitions Troy could make.** Collaborative Literacy (Center for the Collaborative Classroom) retains constructivist/workshop DNA from its Developmental Studies Center predecessor; bolting UFLI onto a workshop core replicates the same architecture that has failed Troy for 11 years. (Full pressure test in `curriculum-evidence/collab_lit_ufli_pressure_test.md`.)
4. **The case for a knowledge-building comprehensive program (CKLA 3rd Ed, EL Education, or Wit & Wisdom + Geodes) paired with a strong systematic phonics core (CKLA Skills strand, UFLI is acceptable for the phonics piece, or Really Great Reading) is far stronger on the evidence.** (Full evaluation matrix in `curriculum-evidence/curriculum_evaluations.md`.)
5. **The peer-district workbook contains *no* example of a successful balanced-literacy → SoR shift in a similar district — because none of the peers have completed one yet.** The relevant case studies are: Steubenville (OH), Bethlehem (PA), the Mississippi Miracle, Louisiana ELA Guidebooks rollout, NYC Reads, and several Tennessee districts — pulled in `district-profiles/peer_district_cases.md`.

## 6. Open questions for Troy leadership

- What does the K-2 picture look like? (G3 is downstream of K-2 instruction; if M-STEP starts at G3, NWEA MAP / DIBELS / aimswebPlus K-2 internal data is needed.)
- What is the in-classroom fidelity to UoS today? (Did Troy already drift to "balanced + Heggerty + some phonics"? If so, the proposed Collab Lit + UFLI may be lateral, not a shift.)
- What teacher training is planned? (LETRS or equivalent is the strongest mediator of SoR-adoption success in peer-district studies — see Steubenville/Mississippi cases.)
- How will Troy measure success? (Pre-defining the metrics matters; many botched SoR adoptions are botched at evaluation, not adoption.)
