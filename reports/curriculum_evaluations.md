# Evidence-Base Evaluation: 14 K-5 Reading Curricula on the Michigan Section 35m Approved List

**Context:** Troy School District (~12K students, affluent Detroit suburb) is choosing K-5 reading curricula under Michigan's Section 35m evidence-based Tier-1 mandate while transitioning from balanced literacy to Science of Reading (SoR). Question being pressure-tested: Is **Collaborative Literacy + UFLI Foundations** the right call?

## Cross-Cutting Context: How to Read These Ratings

Three independent evidence frames matter:

1. **EdReports gateway ratings** (green/yellow/red across Gateway 1 = Text Quality / Foundational Skills, Gateway 2 = Building Knowledge / Comprehension, Gateway 3 = Usability). EdReports' newer v2.0 rubric (rolled out 2024-2025) explicitly scores SoR alignment, including the absence of three-cueing. [EdReports "Next Generation" launch](https://edreports.org/resources/article/edreports-launches-next-generation-of-curriculum-reviews).
2. **The Reading League "Curriculum Navigation Reports" (CNRs)** — a stricter SoR-only lens that flags "red flag practices" (three-cueing, leveled-text decoding practice, lack of phoneme-grapheme systematicity). [TRL CNR launch announcement](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/the-reading-league-launches-groundbreaking-curriculum-navigation-reports-how-well-do-widely-used-curricula-align-with-the-science-of-reading-302111882.html).
3. **What Works Clearinghouse / Evidence for ESSA** — actual effect sizes from controlled studies. ESSA tiers: Strong, Moderate, Promising, Rationale.

**Critical EdReports caveat for Troy.** A March 2025 APM Reports investigation found EdReports has handed all-green ratings to basal programs (Wonders, myView, Into Reading) whose underlying design experts say is misaligned with cognitive-science evidence ([APM Reports March 6, 2025](https://www.apmreports.org/story/2025/03/06/edreports-reading-curriculum-reviews-science-of-reading); echoed by [Natalie Wexler Substack](https://nataliewexler.substack.com/p/literacy-experts-say-some-edreports)). EdReports' methodology rewards *standards coverage volume* over *cognitive-science fit*. For a district moving out of balanced literacy, EdReports green is necessary but not sufficient.

## 1. 95 Phonics Core Program, First Edition (95 Percent Group)

- **EdReports:** [edreports.org/reports/overview/95-core-phonics-program-2020](https://edreports.org/reports/overview/95-core-phonics-program-2020). Supplemental foundational-skills product; Gateway 2 around 75% — does not earn full all-green.
- **Evidence for ESSA:** **STRONG (Tier 1).** 2021-22 Missouri RCT, 3,000+ K-2 students, Acadience ES = +0.09; 2022-23 Arizona matched study ES = +0.29. [Evidence for ESSA listing](https://www.evidenceforessa.org/program/95-phonics-core-program/); [95 Percent Group press](https://www.95percentgroup.com/insights/95-percent-group-earns-strong-essa-standards/).
- **Reading League CNR:** Reviewed; [publisher response PDF](https://www.thereadingleague.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/95-Phonics-Core-Program-Curriculum-Navigation-Report-Publisher-Response.pdf). Also listed at the [Florida Center for Reading Research](https://fcrr.org/repository/95-phonics-core-program).
- **SoR:** Strong — explicit, systematic phonics with decodables.
- **Verdict:** Solid evidence-based supplemental phonics; not a Tier-1 comprehensive program.

## 7. IMSE Orton-Gillingham Plus (OG+), 2025

- **EdReports:** No full-program review located.
- **What Works Clearinghouse:** WWC reviewed *unbranded* OG-based interventions and **was unable to draw any research-based conclusions** — no studies met WWC evidence standards. [WWC Intervention 737](https://ies.ed.gov/ncee/wwc/Intervention/737); [WWC 2010 report](https://ies.ed.gov/ncee/wwc/Docs/InterventionReports/wwc_ortongill_070110.pdf).
- **Evidence for ESSA:** **PROMISING** for IMSE OG+ specifically; quasi-experimental study with 1,000+ grade 1-3 students, aimswebPlus ORF ES = +0.21. [Evidence for ESSA IMSE OG+](https://www.evidenceforessa.org/program/imse-og/).
- **SoR:** Strong (multisensory, explicit, systematic).
- **Verdict:** Promising evidence; SoR-aligned structurally; often used as Tier-2/3 intervention rather than whole-class Tier-1 core.

- **EdReports:** **ALL GREEN** for W&W K-2 and 3-5; 93% Building Knowledge. [EdReports W&W K-2](https://edreports.org/reports/overview/wit-wisdom-2016-k-2); [Great Minds press](https://greatminds.org/english/blog/witwisdom/wit-wisdom-grades-k-2-from-great-minds-earn-top-edreports-rating).
- **Louisiana DOE:** Tier 1 all grades K-5. [Louisiana DOE review PDF](https://doe.louisiana.gov/docs/default-source/curricular-resources/great-minds-pbc---wit-wisdom-ela-grades-k-5-(without-foundational-skills)-(-2016).pdf?sfvrsn=e03a0e87_5).
- **Massachusetts CURATE:** Reviewed favorably. [MA CURATE W&W report](https://www.doe.mass.edu/instruction/curate/ela-2023-wit-wisdom-k-5.pdf).
- **Knowledge Matters Campaign:** Endorsed. [KM listing](https://knowledgematterscampaign.org/curriculum/wit-and-wisdom/).
- **CRITICAL CAVEAT:** W&W is a knowledge/comprehension program **without foundational skills**. Great Minds directs districts to pair with Fundations, Really Great Reading, or similar. **Geodes** (K-3 decodable trade books, ≥80% decodable, topically aligned to W&W modules) supports application but doesn't replace a phonics core. [Great Minds Geodes](https://greatminds.org/english/geodes); [Geodes/W&W/Fundations integration](https://greatminds.org/english/blog/witwisdom/geodes-wit-wisdom-and-fundations-support-data-driven-differentiated-literacy-instruction).

[Great Minds San Tan Heights case](https://greatminds.org/english/blog/witwisdom/wit-wisdom-drives-reading-growth-impact-on-reading-achievement-at-san-tan-heights-k-8).
- **SoR:** Strong for language-comprehension strand; intentionally agnostic on word-recognition.
- **Verdict:** Top-tier knowledge-building; pairs naturally with UFLI / Fundations / Really Great Reading.

## 11. Story Champs 2.0 (Spencer & Petersen, 2018)

- **EdReports:** Not reviewed (intervention/supplemental, not comprehensive core).
- **Peer-reviewed evidence:** 44+ feasibility/experimental studies. [Language Dynamics Group research](https://languagedynamicsgroup.com/research/); [Petersen et al. individualized narrative intervention](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8715709/); [Large-scale kindergarten RCT in LSHSS](https://pubs.asha.org/doi/10.1044/2021_LSHSS-20-00162); [Spanish-English bilingual DLD study](https://pubs.asha.org/doi/10.1044/2024_LSHSS-23-00121).
- **SoR:** Strong on the language-comprehension strand (oral language/narrative).
- **Verdict:** Evidence-strong **supplemental/intervention** for oral language and narrative skills. Not a Tier-1 core; valuable for MTSS Tier-2/3, ELL, and special education.

## 12. UFLI Foundations, 1st Edition

- **EdReports:** **Not reviewed** — UFLI is a foundational-skills supplement, not a comprehensive K-5 core. This caused notable controversy (Pennsylvania's omission of UFLI from its curriculum list): [Curriculum Insight Project, "Who Killed UFLI?"](https://curriculuminsightproject.substack.com/p/who-killed-ufli-the-curious-case).
- **Peer-reviewed efficacy:** Strong. Lane et al. 2025, *Reading Research Quarterly*, found UFLI Foundations produced effects **exceeding +1.0 SD** on early-literacy outcomes in Florida pilot. [RRQ 2025 study](https://ila.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/rrq.607); [EdWeek "dramatic results" coverage](https://www.edweek.org/teaching-learning/researchers-created-a-phonics-program-with-dramatic-results-how-it-works/2025/01); [WestEd districtwide pilot study](https://www.wested.org/resource/districtwide-pilot-study-of-ufli-foundations/); [SchoolWorks Lab summary](https://schoolworkslab.org/2025/01/27/the-power-of-phonemic-awareness-and-phonics-evidence-from-ufli-foundations/). Effects stronger with greater fidelity.
- **Reading League CNR (Feb 2025):** [TRL UFLI CNR](https://www.thereadingleague.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/02/The-Reading-League-Curriculum-Navigation-Report-UFLI.pdf); [UFLI publisher response](https://www.thereadingleague.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/02/UFLI-Curriculum-Navigation-Report-Publisher-Response.pdf). **Limitations:** (a) phonemic-awareness assessment whole-group only — no individual diagnostic; (b) decodable texts mostly single-syllable, **no guidance for teaching unknown vocabulary**; (c) rich vocabulary and complex syntax intentionally absent (by design — code-mastery program).
- **UFLI's own scope caveat:** "UFLI Foundations is **not a comprehensive language arts program.** For optimal use, the program should be paired with curricula that address language comprehension and written expression." [UFLI Foundations page](https://ufli.education.ufl.edu/foundations/). The Intervention Placement Test is also explicitly *not* a universal screener or progress monitor.
- **SoR:** **Excellent** on word-recognition strand.
- **Verdict for Troy:** Strongest evidence-backed phonics program available, and free. Requires strong knowledge-building/comprehension core partner.

## 14. Collaborative Literacy, 3rd Edition (Center for the Collaborative Classroom)

*Includes Being a Reader (foundations + comprehension) and Being a Writer; lineage: Making Meaning / Being a Reader.*

This is the most consequential entry for Troy's decision.

- **EdReports:** The publicly available review covers the **earlier (2016) edition** at [EdReports Collaborative Literacy overview](https://edreports.org/reports/overview/collaborative-literacy). K-2 = **"Partially Meets Expectations"** for Gateway 1 (Text Quality) and Gateway 2 (Building Knowledge); Gateway 3 (Usability) not rated because G1+G2 did not fully meet. The 2016 product is no longer sold, but EdReports retains reviews because districts still use materials. **The 3rd Edition (2024) has not, to my search, received a full EdReports re-review.** Troy should verify the current EdReports status before adoption. State-level summaries: [Colorado DOE Collaborative Literacy summary](https://www.cde.state.co.us/coloradoliteracy/centerforthecollaborativeclassroomcollaborativeliteracysummary); [Ohio DOE 3rd Ed. approval PDF](https://education.ohio.gov/getattachment/Topics/Learning-in-Ohio/English-Language-Art/Resources-for-English-Language-Arts/High-Quality-Instructional-Materials-in-ELA/Approved-List-of-Core-Curriculum-and-Instructional/CenterfortheCollaborativeClassroom-Collaborative-Literacy-3rd-Edition.pdf.aspx?lang=en-US).

- **Reading League CNR (January 2025):** Strongest available SoR-lens evaluation. [TRL Collaborative Literacy CNR PDF](https://www.thereadingleague.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/The-Reading-League-Curriculum-Navigation-Report-Collaborative-Classroom.pdf); [Collaborative Classroom publisher response](https://www.thereadingleague.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/Collaborative-Classroom-Curriculum-Navigation-Report-Publisher-Response.pdf); [Collaborative Classroom announcement](https://www.collaborativeclassroom.org/news/reading-league-curriculum-navigation-report-collaborative-literacy/). **Headline:** "minimal evidence of red flag practices"; curriculum "meets" or "mostly meets" most criteria K-5. Strengths: integration of reading and writing, multilingual-learner supports, trade-book diversity. **Meaningfully favorable** — a substantive shift from the program's balanced-literacy heritage. But "minimal" is not "zero": more cautious than CKLA/EL Ed verdicts, and legacy practices (Individualized Daily Reading at students' own levels, workshop-model independent reading time) remain culturally adjacent to balanced literacy.

- **Publisher's evolution narrative:** [Being a Reader / SoR support page](https://support.collaborativeclassroom.org/hc/en-us/articles/12610811074580-How-does-Being-a-Reader-align-to-the-Science-of-Reading); [Being a Reader evidence base PDF](https://cdn.collaborativeclassroom.org/evidence-base/being-a-reader.pdf); [3rd Edition page](https://www.collaborativeclassroom.org/programs/collaborative-literacy-third-edition/). Added Sound-Out Support for high-frequency words; reviewed problematic keywords (schwa, voicing): [What's New at Collaborative Classroom](https://support.collaborativeclassroom.org/hc/en-us/articles/16943538073876-What-s-new-at-Collaborative-Classroom).

- **The harder SoR critique:** "Sold a Story" aimed at Lucy Calkins' *Units of Study* and Fountas & Pinnell's *LLI* ([APM Reports Sold a Story](https://features.apmreports.org/sold-a-story/); [APM Reports "At a Loss for Words"](https://www.apmreports.org/episode/2019/08/22/whats-wrong-how-schools-teach-reading)) does not aim at Collaborative Classroom directly — but Collaborative Literacy's *workshop-model independent reading* (students reading "just-right" leveled books) is the structural feature Seidenberg, Hanford, and Wexler critique most consistently. Mark Seidenberg's general critique: "balanced literacy" leaves the amount of explicit phonics up to teacher discretion ([Seidenberg's site](https://www.seidenbergreading.net/); [Reading Rockets profile](https://www.readingrockets.org/people-and-organizations/mark-seidenberg)). The Reading League's [Curriculum Evaluation Guidelines 2023](https://www.thereadingleague.org/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/The-Reading-League-Curriculum-Evaluation-Guidelines-2023.pdf) flag "leveled texts as primary practice material" and "use of cueing systems for word identification" as red flags.

- **District adoption outcomes:** Published outcomes are largely **publisher-collected case studies**, not independent peer-reviewed efficacy trials. No Evidence for ESSA tier rating; no WWC entry.

- **SoR verdict:** **Improved but still contested.** 3rd Edition is not the same product critics rejected in 2018-2020. The Reading League CNR is genuinely favorable. **But** it remains the only entry on Troy's shortlist whose lineage runs through workshop-model, leveled-reader, balanced-literacy roots, and which lacks independent efficacy-trial evidence comparable to CKLA, EL Education, UFLI, 95 PCP, or Really Great Reading. For a district *transitioning from* balanced literacy, adopting a program with that lineage risks signaling to teachers that legacy practices are acceptable.

## On the Favored Combination: Collaborative Literacy + UFLI Foundations

**Why this combination has internal logic:**
- UFLI = strongest free phonics evidence (RRQ 2025 ES >1.0 SD).
- Collaborative Literacy 3rd Ed. has improved enough to earn TRL's "minimal red flags" verdict.
- Collaborative Classroom's strengths in writing, multilingual-learner supports, and trade-book breadth are real.

**Why it is the weakest of the comprehensive choices on this list:**
1. **Evidence asymmetry.** CKLA, EL Ed, and Wit & Wisdom carry stronger independent verification (Knowledge Matters endorsement, EdReports v2.0 all-green on current editions, published external district MAP/state-test outcomes). Collaborative Literacy's outcomes are mostly publisher-collected.
2. **Redundancy.** Collaborative Literacy includes its own foundational-skills component (Being a Reader word study + shared reading). Overlaying UFLI creates schedule/sequence conflicts that CKLA, EL Ed, and W&W+RGR/Fundations don't have.
3. **Cultural-transition signal.** Troy is *exiting* balanced literacy. Of the comprehensive programs on the Section 35m list, Collaborative Literacy is closest in design DNA to what Troy is leaving. Adopting it risks teachers reading the change as cosmetic.
4. **UFLI's known limitations** (no vocabulary instruction, no individual phonemic-awareness assessment, no complex-text exposure) are **not solved** by Collaborative Literacy's design; both have lighter footprints on rich-knowledge content sequencing than CKLA.

**Stronger alternatives on the same Michigan Section 35m list:**
- **Amplify CKLA 3rd Edition** (standalone) — solves foundations + knowledge in one program with strongest district-outcomes evidence.
- **EL Education (Imagine Learning)** (standalone) — comparable to CKLA; arguably stronger writing component.
- **Wit & Wisdom + UFLI Foundations + Geodes** — retains UFLI but pairs with strongest knowledge-building comprehension partner.
- **Wit & Wisdom + Really Great Reading + Geodes** — if Troy wants a paid phonics product with stronger EdReports v2.0 standing than UFLI's lack of EdReports review.

## Bottom-Line Recommendations for Troy

1. **Strongest single adoption:** Amplify CKLA 3rd Edition. Most evidence, most coherent K-5 knowledge sequence, integrated foundations, strongest case-study outcomes, signals decisive SoR commitment.
2. **Strongest "comprehension + best-of-breed phonics" combination:** Wit & Wisdom + Geodes + (UFLI Foundations *or* Really Great Reading *or* 95 PCP). If Troy truly wants UFLI, this is the combination that uses it well.
3. **EL Education (Imagine Learning)** is essentially tied with #1.
4. **The Collaborative Literacy + UFLI plan is defensible but suboptimal.** It is not academically negligent — the Reading League CNR is real and favorable — but on this 14-program list it is the weakest comprehensive option for a district moving *out of* balanced literacy. The legacy-of-design problem cannot be solved by overlaying UFLI.
5. **Whatever the core decision, also consider:** Story Champs 2.0 for MTSS Tier 2/3 oral-language support (valuable for ELL and special ed); IMSE OG+ training for dyslexia-targeted teacher capacity; 95 Phonics Core or Magnetic Reading Foundations as supplemental K-2 phonics if not using UFLI.
