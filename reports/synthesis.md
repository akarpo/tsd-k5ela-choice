# Troy SD K-5 ELA Curriculum Evaluation — Synthesis Report

**Question being pressure-tested:** Is **Collaborative Literacy 3rd Ed. + UFLI Foundations** the right K-5 ELA choice for Troy's balanced-literacy → Science of Reading transition?

**One-line answer:** **No.** It is defensible but the weakest of Troy's comprehensive options on the Michigan Section 35m list, and *zero* of the 20 districts nationally that the Education Scorecard 2026 identified as having outperformed their demographics in ELA use Collaborative Literacy. The three programs most-cited by ELA outperformers — **Wit & Wisdom, Amplify CKLA, EL Education** — are all on Troy's "stronger alternatives" shortlist.

## The three datasets, in one picture

### Dataset 1 — Troy's own data (`tsd-achievement.karpowitsch.org` workbook)

Troy has been on **Lucy Calkins Units of Study (Fall 2014)** for 11 years — the most-criticized balanced-literacy program in the U.S. and the central villain of APM Reports' "Sold a Story."

| Grade 3 ELA M-STEP — % Proficient/Advanced | 2019 | 2024 (trough) | 2025 | Δ 2019→2025 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| All Students | 74.0% | 63.2% | 67.9% | **-6.1** |
| Asian | 80.3% | 71.2% | 72.9% | **-7.4** |
| White | 73.4% | 59.5% | 70.1% | -3.3 |
| Students with Disabilities | 38.0% | 25.6% | 27.6% | **-10.4** |
| English Learners | 63.2% | 45.7% | 54.3% | -8.9 |

**Three findings that drive everything else:**

1. **The Asian subgroup itself is in free-fall (-7.4 pts).** Of the 8 self-selected peer districts in Troy's own workbook, Troy's Asian G3 cohort has the **worst trend**. Coppell ISD (TX, similar demographics, in-house balanced literacy) *gained* 7 points over the same window. The "ESL composition" narrative is not supported by the peer comparison.
2. **White G3 bottomed at 59.5% in 2024.** For an affluent suburb, this is the most damning number on the page. White students underperform Asian students at Troy in every year shown — this is *not* a demographics story.
3. **Grade 4 dropped 10.7 points and Grade 6 dropped 10.4 points.** The "reading-to-learn" transition is where knowledge-light balanced literacy fails most visibly.

### Dataset 3 — Sustained outperformers (10-25 years), Steubenville-style

Full analysis: `district-profiles/sustained_outperformers.md`. These are a *different* evidence class than recent gainers — they tell us what holds up over the long run. 17 districts documented; curriculum frequency during their sustained-performance window:

| Approach | Count | Districts |
|---|---|---|
| Success for All | 1 | Steubenville (25 yrs, ~80% F/RL, 93-100% G3 reading proficient) |
| Open Court / explicit-phonics basal | 3 | Garden Grove, Long Beach (Cohn era), Sanger |
| CKLA / Core Knowledge | 1 | Aldine (post-2020 upgrade) |
| Bookworms | 1 | Seaford DE (exited BL to succeed) |
| District-built structured scope+sequence | 5 | Brownsville, Lane OK, Cottonwood OK, Gwinnett AKS, Houston (Paige) |
| **Balanced literacy / Workshop as core** | **3** | **Valley Stream 30 (current), Boston/Payzant (not sustained), NYC CSD 2/Alvarado (not sustained)** |
| Mixed / district-built with BL practices | 3 | Norfolk, Charlotte-Meck, Miami-Dade |

**Only 1 of 17 sustained outperformers currently uses balanced literacy as a core:** Valley Stream 30 NY — a small (1,800-student), mid-income, demographically-favorable Long Island district that pairs F&P with daily explicit phonics. The two other BL cases (Boston/Payzant, NYC CSD 2/Alvarado) **peaked and flattened** within 5-10 years; Boston is now moving to CKLA/EL Education.

Three additional findings:

1. **Long Beach USD is the cleanest natural experiment in U.S. K-5 ELA.** Sustained outperformance under structured curriculum during the Cohn era (1992-2002, Open Court / Reading First-aligned, Broad Prize 2003); flattening after the 2010s drift to **Teachers College Units of Study** (Calkins). Same district, same insider leadership culture ("the Long Beach Way"), same demographics — *only the curriculum changed*. This is the closest thing to a controlled trial against balanced literacy that exists in U.S. K-5 ELA, and it points against the program Troy is currently running.

2. **Aldine ISD ate its own laurels.** Won the Broad Prize in 2009 on a district-built structured approach; in 2020 a literacy task force found only 29% of 3rd graders proficient on STAAR; the district *upgraded to Amplify CKLA* — first Texas district to adopt at scale. A Broad-Prize winner chose CKLA as the upgrade path.

3. **Seaford DE is the cleanest "exit BL to succeed" story.** Last in Delaware in 2015 (32% SBAC); explicitly exited BL-ish practices for **Bookworms** (open-source, U Delaware's Sharon Walpole); reached ~53% by 2019 and held ~41% through COVID. Peer-reviewed UDel study (May, Strong, Walpole, *Scientific Studies in Reading*) confirms compounding K-5 gains.

## Why Collaborative Literacy + UFLI is the wrong answer

Full critique: `curriculum-evidence/collab_lit_ufli_pressure_test.md`. The three structural problems no amount of PD can fix:

1. **The core is still strategy-and-leveled-text in grades 3-5.** Making Meaning is the most balanced-literacy-DNA component of Collaborative Literacy. Bolting UFLI onto K-2 phonics doesn't change Grade 3-5 instruction. Troy's worst grade-level declines are at **Grade 4 (-10.7) and Grade 6 (-10.4)** — exactly where Collab Lit's design has the weakest grip.
2. **It is not a coherent knowledge-building curriculum.** The Knowledge Matters Campaign's recognized knowledge-builders are CKLA, EL Ed, Wit & Wisdom, ARC Core, Bookworms, and Fishtank. **Collaborative Literacy is not on that list.** Troy's 4th- and 6th-grade collapse is the canonical knowledge-gap pattern Natalie Wexler documents — the half of SoR (language comprehension via background knowledge) that UFLI cannot address.
3. **UFLI duplicates Being a Reader's K-2 phonics.** BaR's Small-Group Reading Sets 1-6 share scope and sequence with SIPPS — Collaborative Classroom's own systematic phonics program. Layering UFLI on top creates schedule conflicts and signals that the BaR foundational strand isn't trusted. If it isn't trusted, don't buy it.

**National validation across two independent datasets:**
- Of 20 demographic-adjusted ELA outperformers in the Education Scorecard 2026 (post-COVID gainers), **zero** use Collaborative Literacy as their K-5 core.
- Of 17 sustained outperformers (10-25 years of beating demographic expectations), **only 1 of 17 currently uses balanced literacy as a core** — Valley Stream 30 NY, a small advantaged district that pairs F&P with daily explicit phonics. The two other BL cases (Boston/Payzant, NYC CSD 2/Alvarado) **peaked and flattened**.

If Troy adopts Collab Lit + UFLI, it will be the *only* district in either dataset using a workshop-derived core with a phonics bolt-on. **The Long Beach USD natural experiment** — sustained outperformance under structured curriculum (Cohn era), flattening after the drift to Teachers College Units of Study — controls for district, leadership, and demographics. Only the curriculum changed, and the performance changed with it. Troy is on the same TC Units of Study Long Beach drifted into.

## What Troy should do instead

### Recommended primary path
**Adopt Amplify CKLA 3rd Edition K-5.** Lowest implementation complexity (single vendor, integrated foundations + knowledge), strongest EdReports rating (first ELA foundational-skills program to earn all-green), strongest independent district outcomes (Casa Grande AZ K reading proficiency tripled; Charleston SC +5.6% G3 in year one; Richland WA +45% reading proficiency in year one; Fond du Lac WI #1 statewide reading growth). Direct in-state precedent at neighboring Marion County KY (Amplify CKLA + LETRS → +10% G3-7 reading proficiency).

### Strong alternative
**Wit & Wisdom + Wilson Fundations K-3 + Geodes K-3.** Best-of-breed combination. Largest urban proof-point (Baltimore City). Direct affluent-suburban proof-point (Roanoke County VA — closest demographic match to Troy). One-year-to-results proof-point (West Baton Rouge LA, 57% → 68%).

### Honorable mention
**EL Education K-5** (with a phonics supplement like Fundations or 95 Phonics). In-state Michigan validator (Detroit DPSCD). NYC Reads natural experiment found District 11 (EL Ed) outperformed both Into Reading and Wit & Wisdom districts.

### If political/buy-in constraints force a Collab Lit landing
The least-bad version of the proposed combo:
- Keep Collab Lit as a transitional core
- **Drop Making Meaning in grades 3-5**; substitute a knowledge-building supplement (Fishtank ELA modules, W&W modules)
- Use **SIPPS for tier-2 only**, **UFLI as the tier-1 K-2 phonics** to avoid duplication
- Commit publicly to a full review in 2-3 years

This is a holding pattern, not a destination.

## What every outperformer got right, regardless of curriculum

The Education Scorecard outperformers, peer-district cases, and Troy's own data all converge on the same five execution factors. Curriculum is necessary but not sufficient.

1. **Knowledge-building core + systematic phonics + teacher training, as a stack.** Palo Alto USD, Bethlehem Area SD, Steubenville, Mississippi, Louisiana — every long-run winner paired all three.
2. **Sustained, structured teacher PD — and LETRS is the most-cited brand, not a universal.** Of the 20 Education Scorecard outperformers, **3 explicitly named LETRS** (Modesto CA, Marion County KY, Sikeston MO); 2-3 more inherit it implicitly via state mandates (MS, possibly LA). The remainder used different branded PD: Rollins Center (Atlanta), modEL Detroit/StandardsWork (Detroit), Data-Driven Instruction (Fond du Lac), TN Reading 360 (Johnson City), TX STR (Spring Branch), CT Right-to-Read (East Hartford), Orton-Gillingham mandate (Palo Alto peer). Mississippi's "Miracle" *is* fundamentally a LETRS story at the state level; Modesto's +13 weeks reading gain came from LETRS, not Benchmark Advance. **The universal pattern is 60+ hours of sustained, structured PD per K-5 teacher across years 1-2 with coaching in year 3+, regardless of brand — LETRS is the safest default and the most thoroughly studied.**
3. **Universal screeners + MTSS.** Acadience, DIBELS, i-Ready — every outperformer has K-2 universal screening with intervention triggers. Troy should pair this with a M-STEP-G3-leading internal predictor.
4. **A 2-3 year lag is normal.** NC LETRS 2021 → flat NAEP 2024. Bethlehem K change 2015 → 3rd-grade bounce 2018. Plan board comms on a 5-year horizon.
5. **Fidelity is the binding constraint.** NYC Reads Phase 1 teacher confidence was 55%; Phase 2 was 38% — a 17-point gap that translates directly into student outcomes. Teachers will mix old materials with new under time pressure. Plan for fidelity coaching, not just adoption.
