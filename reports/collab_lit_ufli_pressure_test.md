# Pressure-Testing Collaborative Literacy (3rd ed.) + UFLI Foundations for Troy School District's K-5 ELA Shift

**Question:** Is Collaborative Literacy 3rd Edition (Center for the Collaborative Classroom) + UFLI Foundations the strongest evidence-based choice for a balanced-literacy → SoR shift? Or is it a compromise that preserves balanced-literacy DNA with a phonics bolt-on?

---

## 1. What is actually in Collaborative Literacy, 3rd Edition?

CCC's Collaborative Literacy is composed of three strands:
- **Being a Reader (BaR)** — foundational skills + whole-class reading K-5; Small-Group Sets 1-6 use decodables, Sets 7-12 use leveled trade books
- **Making Meaning (MM)** — read-aloud-driven comprehension strategy instruction K-6
- **Being a Writer (BaW)** — writers'-workshop-style writing K-6

Being a Writer 3rd Ed. released 2024; the broader "Third Edition" Collaborative Literacy refresh accompanies it ([CCC – Collab Lit 3rd Ed.](https://www.collaborativeclassroom.org/programs/collaborative-literacy-third-edition/); [Press release 2024](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/collaborative-classroom-publishes-new-third-edition-of-being-a-writer-a-comprehensive-k5-writing-curriculum-302163641.html)).

**Phonics scope and sequence (1a):** BaR's foundational strand shares its scope and sequence with **SIPPS** (CCC's standalone phonics program). CCC: *"Being a Reader Small-Group Reading Sets share aligned scopes and sequences and teaching routines with SIPPS"* ([CCC – BaR SGRS](https://www.collaborativeclassroom.org/programs/being-a-reader-small-group-reading-sets/)). SIPPS is rated **Strong** by Evidence for ESSA and **Tier 1** in Louisiana ([Evidence for ESSA – SIPPS](https://www.evidenceforessa.org/program/sipps-systematic-instruction-in-phonological-awareness-phonics-and-sight-words-2/); [LA DOE](https://doe.louisiana.gov/school-system-leaders/instructional-materials-reviews)). The phonics is **systematic**, not incidental.

**Decodable vs leveled / MSV (1b):** Sets 1-6 use decodables; Sets 7-12 transition to **leveled trade books**. Making Meaning is built almost entirely around leveled read-alouds and student-selected leveled independent reading. The 3rd ed. integrates decodables in early Sets, then transitions to a leveled-text model that resembles guided reading. The Reading League's Jan 2025 Curriculum Navigation Report found *"minimal evidence of red flag practices"* and rated most criteria "meets/mostly meets" K-5 ([Reading League Report PDF](https://www.thereadingleague.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/The-Reading-League-Curriculum-Navigation-Report-Collaborative-Classroom.pdf); [CCC announcement](https://www.collaborativeclassroom.org/news/reading-league-curriculum-navigation-report-collaborative-literacy/)) — meaningful, but "minimal" is not "zero," and reviewers noted issues with Spelling-Sound Chart key word choices and emphasis on independent reading in K-1 before students have sufficient word-attack skills.

**Knowledge-building (1c) — the decisive distinction.** Collaborative Literacy is **not** a coherent knowledge-building curriculum in the CKLA/EL/W&W sense. CCC: *"selected units at each grade level focusing on science or social studies topics"* ([CCC – BaR](https://www.collaborativeclassroom.org/programs/being-a-reader/)). The model is **thematic and strategy-driven**, not domain-sequenced. The Knowledge Matters Campaign's recognized knowledge-building curricula are **ARC Core, Bookworms, CKLA, EL Education, Fishtank ELA, and Wit & Wisdom** ([KMC Directory](https://knowledgematterscampaign.org/explore-curricula/)). **Collaborative Literacy is not on that list.**

**Comprehension instruction (1d):** Making Meaning is the classic balanced-literacy strategy menu: *"Each week begins with a read-aloud lesson... students practice using the strategy with a very familiar text"* ([CCC blog – Making Meaning](https://www.collaborativeclassroom.org/blog/what-a-gem-the-unique-approach-to-the-daily-lessons-in-making-meaning/)). Tim Shanahan has been clear: ~6 weeks of strategy instruction is enough; over-emphasizing strategies over knowledge and text analysis is a known weakness of this lineage ([Shanahan – How Much Comp Strategy Instruction?](https://www.readingrockets.org/blogs/shanahan-on-literacy/how-much-comprehension-strategy-instruction); [Shanahan – Teaching Reading Comprehension](https://www.shanahanonliteracy.com/blog/teaching-reading-comprehension-and-comprehension-strategies)).

**Writing (1e):** Being a Writer 3rd Ed. is a **writers'-workshop model** — Calkins/Atwell lineage. Not the explicit, sentence-level, knowledge-integrated writing of The Writing Revolution or the writing strands embedded in CKLA/W&W.

## 2. What experts/journalists/reviewers say

**EdReports (2a):** Collaborative Literacy (2016) earns **"Partially Meets Expectations"** on Gateway 1 (Text Quality) and Gateway 2 (Building Knowledge) across K, 2, 3, 5 ([Grade 2](https://www.edreports.org/reports/detail/collaborative-literacy-2); [Grade 3](https://www.edreports.org/reports/detail/collaborative-literacy/third-grade); [K](https://www.edreports.org/reports/detail/collaborative-literacy/kindergarten); [Grade 5](https://edreports.org/reports/detail/collaborative-literacy/fifth-grade)). Direct quote (Grade 2): *"the Grade 2 instructional materials partially meet expectations for Gateway 1... partially meet the expectations of Gateway 2... materials partially meet criteria that texts are organized to support students' building knowledge of different topics."* **The 3rd ed. has not yet been re-reviewed under EdReports' June 2024 K-2 SoR foundational-skills criteria** ([EdReports – New K-2 Reviews](https://edreports.org/resources/article/edreports-launches-new-ela-k-2-foundational-skills-curriculum-reviews-to-better-align-with-science-of-reading-research-and-policy)) — the new criteria explicitly require *"explicit, systematic instruction of research-based... phonics"* and that *"materials are expected to be absent of the three-cueing system."*

For comparison:
- **Amplify CKLA (K-5)** — **all-green**, first ELA foundational skills program to earn it ([Amplify announcement](https://amplify.com/news/amplify-ckla-skills-becomes-first-ela-foundational-skills-program-to-earn-all-green-scores-from-edreports/); [EdReports – CKLA](https://edreports.org/reports/overview/core-knowledge-language-arts-ckla-2015))
- **EL Education K-5 (2017)** — Meets Expectations on G1/G2/G3 every grade K-5 (older tool) ([EdReports – EL Education](https://edreports.org/reports/overview/el-education-k-5-language-arts-2017))
- **Wit & Wisdom** — high marks on G1-3 (not a foundational-skills program; pairs with Fundations/Geodes)

**The Reading League (2b):** Jan 2025 report on Collab Lit was **positive overall** — "meet/mostly meet most criteria K-5" with "minimal red flags." Commended R/W integration, multilingual supports, trade book diversity, PD videos. **But** Navigation Reports are explicitly **"not recommendations"** — alignment ≠ overall quality vs. alternatives ([Report](https://www.thereadingleague.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/The-Reading-League-Curriculum-Navigation-Report-Collaborative-Classroom.pdf); [Publisher Response](https://www.thereadingleague.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/Collaborative-Classroom-Curriculum-Navigation-Report-Publisher-Response.pdf)).

**Emily Hanford / "Sold a Story" (2c):** Hanford's series targeted **Units of Study (Calkins), Fountas & Pinnell, Reading Recovery** ([APM – Sold a Story finale](https://www.apmreports.org/episode/2024/04/04/sold-a-story-e9-the-aftermath)). She has **not specifically named Collaborative Literacy**. But the broader critique — independent reading of leveled text + strategy-based comprehension — applies to the lineage. Heinemann's sales fell **~75% from 2019-2023** ([APM – Heinemann financial trouble](https://www.apmreports.org/story/2024/04/30/publisher-heinemann-financial-trouble-science-of-reading)). NYC, Philly, SF, Louisville, Portland OR, Loudoun County VA replaced Heinemann; replacements are CKLA/EL/W&W, not leveled-text successors.

**Natalie Wexler (2d):** *The Knowledge Gap* and *Beyond the Science of Reading* (ASCD, 2025): comprehension is a function of background knowledge built through coherent content sequence. She endorses the KMC six. Collaborative Literacy is not in that group; her framework would call its thematic-strategy structure insufficient ([Wexler – Knowledge Gap](https://nataliewexler.com/the-knowledge-gap/); [Wexler Substack](https://nataliewexler.substack.com/p/is-the-buzz-about-knowledge-justified)).

**Tim Shanahan (2e):** Recommends roughly equal time on words/word parts, fluency, comprehension, writing. Warns against over-emphasizing strategy instruction and against siloed phonics — pushes back on both ends of Collab Lit + UFLI.

**Mark Seidenberg (2f):** Critique aimed at education-school traditions, not specific curricula. Supportive of SIPPS/UFLI-style systematic phonics; skeptical of leveled-text instruction past the decodable phase ([Seidenberg Reading](https://seidenbergreading.net/)).

**Achieve the Core / Student Achievement Partners (2g):** Publishes the IMET and maintains **CKLA Adaptation Packs**. No equivalent supports for Collaborative Literacy ([Achieve the Core – SAP](https://achievethecore.org/author/2/student-achievement-partners)).

## 3. Publisher lineage

CCC was founded as **Developmental Studies Center (DSC)** in 1980 by **Eric Schaps**. DSC merged with **Cornerstone Literacy, Inc.** in 2015 ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Center_for_the_Collaborative_Classroom)). Schaps's foundational research was on **SEL, classroom community, cooperative learning** — "positive school affinity and supportive personal relationships" ([CCC – Schaps memorial](https://www.collaborativeclassroom.org/blog/remembering-eric-schaps-1942-2021/)). The pedagogical DNA is **socio-constructivist and cooperative-learning-oriented**, not structured-literacy-oriented. SIPPS is the structured-literacy strand layered into the constructivist core. The 3rd edition is a meaningful **refresh, not a rebuild** — CCC has not abandoned the workshop model.

## 4. UFLI Foundations

**Strengths:** Explicit, systematic K-2 phonics program from UF/Holly Lane (2022). WestEd districtwide pilot (21 schools): K-2 students gained ~8 months extra instruction in K, ~1.5 years in Grade 1, effect sizes >1.0 SD ([WestEd](https://www.wested.org/resource/districtwide-pilot-study-of-ufli-foundations/); [Lane et al., 2025 RRQ](https://ila.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/rrq.607); [EdWeek – "Dramatic Results"](https://www.edweek.org/teaching-learning/researchers-created-a-phonics-program-with-dramatic-results-how-it-works/2025/01)). Manual ~$80; resources largely free.

**Limitations:**
- **K-2 only as core.** UFLI: *"intended for core instruction for students in Kindergarten-2nd grade and intervention for 3rd grade and up"* ([UFLI Foundations](https://ufli.education.ufl.edu/foundations/)). **Not a tier-1 phonics core for grades 3-5.** For grades 3-5, Troy would rely on BaR Sets or layer in advanced word study elsewhere.
- **Phonics only.** No fluency depth, vocabulary, comprehension, or writing.
- **Thin decodable text.** Short controlled passages embedded in lessons; districts supplement.
- **30-minute lessons.** Focused block, not a comprehensive literacy program.

**Vs. alternatives:** Amplify CKLA Skills (K-2) is the only ELA foundational program to earn EdReports all-green. Wilson Fundations is the OG-based K-3 multisensory gold standard (pairs with Geodes for 80%+ decodable connected text). 95 Phonics Core has K-5 scope. SIPPS — **already inside BaR** — is Strong/Tier 1 evidence.

**Critical observation:** Collab Lit + UFLI **duplicates K-2 phonics**. BaR Sets 1-6 + SIPPS routines already provide systematic K-2 phonics. UFLI on top is either redundant or a tacit admission that BaR's foundational strand isn't trusted — in which case why pay for it?

## 5. The bolting problem

EdWeek (Aug 2025) on supplementing high-quality cores: *"using bits and pieces from different curricula without a clear strategy in place could lead to all of them being less effective"* ([EdWeek](https://www.edweek.org/teaching-learning/districts-using-high-quality-reading-curricula-still-supplement-with-other-materials-why/2025/08)). Risk profile of Collab Lit + UFLI:
1. **Foundational duplication** (BaR + SIPPS + UFLI = three overlapping K-2 phonics sequences)
2. **Making Meaning stays** in 3-5 — the most balanced-literacy-DNA component (leveled read-alouds, strategy instruction, leveled independent reading)
3. **No knowledge-building gain** — UFLI is mute; Collab Lit is thematic, not coherent
4. **Writing remains workshop** — same lineage as Calkins Units of Study writing

Seattle Schools' CCC implementation study (2021) documented teachers compressing/omitting components under time pressure when programs are layered ([Seattle memo PDF](https://www.seattleschools.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/Memo_SiteVisitFindings_final_deidentified_ADA.pdf)).

## 6. The strongest alternatives

| Criterion | **CKLA K-5** | **EL Ed + phonics** | **W&W + Fundations + Geodes** | **CollabLit + UFLI** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| EdReports K-5 all-green | **Yes** | Yes (older tool) | Yes (W&W) | **No (Partially Meets)** |
| EdReports new K-2 SoR | **All-green (CKLA Skills)** | Mixed | Fundations strong | **Not re-reviewed** |
| Knowledge Matters listed | **Yes** | Yes | Yes | **No** |
| Coherent K-5 knowledge sequence | **Yes (domain)** | Yes (modules) | Yes (modules) | **Thematic only** |
| Systematic K-2 phonics integrated | **Yes** | Yes (Skills Block) | Yes (Fundations) | Yes + UFLI redundant |
| K-5 systematic word study | Yes (Skills 3-5) | Yes | Via Fundations | BaR 7-12 leveled |
| Decodable text post-K2 | Integrated | Limited | **Geodes (80%+)** | Leveled trade books |
| Writing evidence-aligned | Embedded explicit | Strong embedded | Strong embedded | Workshop (BaW) |
| Balanced-lit DNA risk | Low | Low | Low | **High** |

**Option A — Amplify CKLA, K-5:** All-green EdReports K-5; ESSA Tier I-Strong; KMC-recognized; FCRR efficacy gains in one semester ([FCRR](https://fcrr.org/projects/efficacy-core-knowledge-language-arts-read-aloud-program-kindergarten-through-second-grade)). Lafourche Parish LA: 73% basic-or-better on G3 LEAP. Tennessee "Southern Surge" — CKLA/W&W/EL districts drove top-of-nation NAEP G4/G8 growth ([Chalkbeat – Southern Surge](https://www.chalkbeat.org/2025/10/28/lessons-from-the-southern-surge-on-naep/); [Karen Vaites – Southern Surge](https://www.karenvaites.org/p/the-southern-surge-understanding); [Amplify Research](https://amplify.com/research-and-case-studies/amplify-ckla-research/)). Single publisher, integrated K-2 Skills + K-5 Knowledge.

**Option B — EL Education K-5 + phonics supplement:** Meets Expectations EdReports K-5; KMC-recognized. K-2 Skills Block less explicit than CKLA Skills/Fundations — most districts supplement.

**Option C — W&W + Fundations + Geodes:** High EdReports marks on W&W; Fundations is structured-literacy gold standard K-3; Geodes is 80%+ decodable to Fundations scope. Haverhill MA: ELA Proficiency +4pp first year; San Tan Heights AZ (Johns Hopkins study): interim scores rose every year, largest gains for below-proficiency students ([San Tan Heights](https://greatminds.org/english/blog/witwisdom/wit-wisdom-drives-reading-growth-impact-on-reading-achievement-at-san-tan-heights-k-8); [Haverhill](https://greatminds.org/english/blog/witwisdom/grassroots-enthusiasm-powers-adoption-of-evidence-based-ela-and-math-curricula)).

**Option D (proposed) — Collab Lit + UFLI:** Older edition "Partially Meets" G1/G2 at K/2/3/5; 3rd ed. not re-reviewed under new SoR criteria. Reading League "mostly meets" + "minimal red flags." **Not on KMC list.** Limited published 3rd ed. outcome data.

---

## 8. Verdict

**No. Collaborative Literacy 3rd ed. + UFLI Foundations is not the strongest evidence-based choice for a balanced-literacy → SoR shift.** It is a defensible step forward, and The Reading League's Jan 2025 report indicates CCC has done substantive alignment work — but three structural problems no amount of PD can fix:

1. **The core is still strategy-and-leveled-text in grades 3-5.** Making Meaning is the most balanced-literacy-DNA component. Bolting UFLI onto K-2 phonics doesn't change what happens in grades 3-5.
2. **It is not a coherent knowledge-building curriculum.** The KMC consensus six — CKLA, EL Ed, W&W, ARC Core, Bookworms, Fishtank — exclude Collaborative Literacy. The knowledge-building half of SoR is unaddressed.
3. **UFLI duplicates BaR/SIPPS.** If you don't trust BaR's foundational strand, don't add UFLI — adopt a core whose foundational instruction you do trust.

**Recommendation:**
- **First choice:** Amplify CKLA K-5 (lowest implementation complexity; all-green EdReports; ESSA Tier I; KMC; Southern Surge outcome data).
- **Strong modular alternative:** Wit & Wisdom (K-5) + Fundations (K-3) + Geodes. Best-in-class on each component; published district outcome data.
- **Honorable mention:** EL Education K-5 + a phonics supplement (Fundations, 95 Phonics, or UFLI).

**Least-bad version of the proposed combo** (if political/buy-in considerations make a clean break impossible): keep Collab Lit as transitional core, **drop Making Meaning in grades 3-5** in favor of a knowledge-building supplement (Fishtank ELA, W&W modules, etc.), use **SIPPS for tier-2 only** and **UFLI as tier-1 K-2 phonics** to avoid duplication, commit publicly to a full review in 2-3 years. This is a holding pattern, not a destination.
