# Methodology

## Primary metric

**SEDA cohort-standardized score (cs)** from the Stanford Education Data Archive v2025.1. The cs metric expresses every state's test results in NAEP-anchored grade-level units:

- `0.0` = at the national grade-level performance norm
- `+1.0` = approximately one full grade level above national norm
- `−1.0` = approximately one full grade level below national norm

This is the only metric that is genuinely **cross-state comparable in absolute terms**. It normalizes M-STEP (MI), CAASPP (CA), STAAR (TX), LEAP (LA), MAAP (MS), TCAP (TN), NJSLA (NJ), SBA (WA), OST (OH), and every other state's test against a common NAEP scale.

Reference: Reardon, S. F., Ho, A. D., Shear, B. R., Fahle, E. M., Kalogrides, D., saliba, j. (2026). Stanford Education Data Archive (Version 2025.1). https://purl.stanford.edu/

## Pre-COVID → Post-COVID Δ

- **Pre-COVID window:** 2017, 2018, 2019 (3-year mean)
- **Post-COVID window:** 2022, 2023, 2024, 2025 (4-year mean)
- **Δ:** post-COVID mean − pre-COVID mean (in grade-level units)

2020 and 2021 are omitted because state assessments were canceled or partially administered nationwide. This is the SEDA team's standard recovery-window convention.

## Grade and subject scope

- **Grades:** 3, 4, 5 (pooled into a single district × year value, weighted by tested-student count per grade)
- **Subject:** RLA (Reading/Language Arts) only. Math excluded.

## Subgroup definitions

SEDA reports cs values for these subgroups:

| Code | SEDA column | Subgroup |
|---|---|---|
| `all` | `cs_mn_all` | All students |
| `asn` | `cs_mn_asn` | Asian |
| `blk` | `cs_mn_blk` | Black or African American |
| `hsp` | `cs_mn_hsp` | Hispanic or Latino |
| `wht` | `cs_mn_wht` | White |
| `ecd` | `cs_mn_ecd` | Economically Disadvantaged |
| `nec` | `cs_mn_nec` | Not Economically Disadvantaged |

Subgroups with fewer than 10 tested students per cell are suppressed by SEDA per FERPA. Where a district has consistent suppression in early years (e.g., Troy Hispanic subgroup pre-2011), the analysis uses the earliest available year as baseline and notes the asterisk.

## Pooling logic

For each (district, year) combination, G3-G5 cs scores per subgroup are pooled using n-weighted mean:

```
pooled_score = Σ (cs_grade × n_tested_grade) / Σ (n_tested_grade)
```

This handles the case where Texas (for example) had only G5 data pre-2017 — the pooled mean for those years just reflects G5, while later years reflect G3-G5 combined.

## State-relative position

For three districts shown explicitly on slide 11 (Marion County KY, Fond du Lac WI, Troy SD MI), we report:

```
state_relative(district, year) = district_cs(year) − state_cs(year)
```

Computed from the SEDA 2025.1 state-level long file. State-relative position controls for any state-test recalibration drift and shows whether a district is keeping pace with, gaining on, or losing ground to its state's average.

## Cross-state comparability caveat

The cs scale handles cross-state comparison well **on aggregate**. But two limitations remain:

1. **Subgroup small-N suppression varies** across states. Comparing Black-subgroup Δ at Bloomfield Hills (Black subgroup ~10% of students) to Black-subgroup Δ at Bethlehem PA (Black subgroup ~11%) is valid in principle, but small N drives larger error bars.

2. **State test scale revisions** during 2017-2025 should be absorbed by the SEDA cohort-standardization process, but introduce noise. The 2014-15 Michigan MEAP→M-STEP transition is the clearest example — Troy's +0.30 single-year jump in 2015 likely reflects the test transition more than instructional change.

## Where the data lives

- **Raw SEDA bulk files:** `data/_raw_seda_*.csv` (not committed — too large; download via `analysis/extract_seda_subset.py`)
- **Extracted subset:** `data/seda_2025_pooled.json` (50 districts × 13 years × 7 subgroups)
- **State averages:** `data/seda_2025_state.json`
- **Pre/post Δ matrix:** `data/seda_subgroup_delta.csv` (regenerated by `analysis/compute_deltas.py`)
- **Master cross-state dataset:** `data/master_dataset.csv` (2,544 rows — district × year × grade × subgroup × % proficient on state tests; the raw-percent companion to the SEDA cs data)
- **Charts:** `charts/chart01-19.png` (matplotlib outputs)
