# 01 — Kickoff

**Date:** 2026-04 (early in the project)
**Asked for:** An evidence-based read on whether Troy School District should continue with Lucy Calkins Units of Study or move to Collaborative Literacy + UFLI Foundations, grounded in SEDA cohort-standardized ELA performance and peer-district comparisons.

## Prompt(s) quoted
> Help me analyze the K-5 ELA curriculum choice in front of Troy School District. They use Calkins (adopted 2014) and are weighing Collaborative Literacy + UFLI Foundations. I want a defensible, evidence-based view — not opinions.

## What was produced
- Initial peer-district set anchored to Troy's demographics and 4th-grade reading bands.
- First pass at SEDA cs-scale Pre→Post-COVID deltas across the peer set.
- Early classification: Science of Reading vs Balanced Literacy curricula by district.
- The skeleton of what later became the executive deck.

## Why this mattered
This framed the whole project: every later slide, dataset, and design decision derives from the question "what does the evidence actually say about Troy's choice?" The constraint *evidence-based, not opinion* is what pushed us toward SEDA (peer-comparable, NAEP-anchored) rather than M-STEP alone, and what later motivated the three-pass numerical audit.
