Background:
Marion County Public Schools (MCPS) serves a rural population in central Kentucky. Under the
leadership of Superintendent Chris Brady, the district has prioritized high-quality instructional
materials and direct student-to-teacher engagement. The district‚Äôs efforts have yielded
significant growth in recent years; between the 2021-22 and 2024-25 school years, students in
grades 3-7 improved by over 10% in the percentage of those scoring proficient or above in
reading, and the percentage of eighth-grade students scoring proficient or above in math surged
from 18% to 50% in the same time period. By transitioning into a content-specific coaching
model and committing to evidence-based training, MCPS has established a rigorous
environment dedicated to long-term student mastery.
