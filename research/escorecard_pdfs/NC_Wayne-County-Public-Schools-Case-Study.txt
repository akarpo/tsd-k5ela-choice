Background:
Wayne County Public Schools (WCPS) operates 33 traditional public schools serving students in
grades K-12 in Goldsboro, North Carolina, approximately 50 miles southeast of Raleigh.
Established 149 years ago, the district has grown to employ approximately 3,000 staff members,
making it the second largest employer in Wayne County.

In the spring of 2023, WCPS was identified by the state as a low-performing school district, with
more than 50% of its schools below state expectations. Dr. Marc Whichard, a North Carolina
superintendent with turnaround experience, was hired that year and began as superintendent on
July 1, 2023. WCPS exited low-performing district status in his first year. By the end of his third
year, the district had climbed from 91st of 115 North Carolina districts to 61st, posted the
highest-growth high school and highest-growth middle school in the state simultaneously, and
saw Eastern Wayne Middle School move from an F to a C in a single year.

The district achieved these gains while operating on a 2023-24 fiscal year state budget, with
North Carolina per-pupil funding ranked among the lowest in the nation and teacher pay the
lowest in the Southeast. District leaders project a continued climb into the low 40s for the
2025-26 results, more than 50 positions higher than three years ago.
